## Overview

Hydey is a flop-eared bunny who runs a shelter for stray toys — and who is, herself, two people. The unstable [TDay](tday) Variant of the [Apotheosis Serum](apotheosis-serum) did something to her it did to almost no one else: rather than hollowing out her empathy or destroying her mind, it *split* her. Most of the time she is gentle, shy, and kind — by any ordinary measure one of the [Tempered](tempered), a giantess who kept her capacity to see toys as people. But the appetite the serum builds into all [the Transformed](the-transformed) did not simply sit alongside that kindness in her, straining against it the way it does in other Tempered. In Hydey it became something separate. When her lust builds past a certain threshold, the gentle giant is gone, and a ravenous monster wears her body in her place.

She named herself, or was named, for the old-world story that fits her exactly: the kind doctor and the brutal thing inside him. Hydey is both, and never at the same time, and the tragedy of her is that the gentle one always comes back to find out what the other one did.

## Appearance

Hydey is a tan-and-brown bunny with long, flop ears that frame her face, medium-length dirty-blonde hair, and soft brown eyes. She stands a little over eight feet — small for a giantess, which only sharpens the contrast she embodies: she does not look like a monster, and in her gentle state she does not carry herself like one, shy and unassuming and easy to mistake for harmless.

The serum left her endowed in a canine form. In her kind phase it is simply part of her, unremarked; in her other state it becomes the instrument of everything the monster does, and — owing to its canine nature — the thing that complicates her return to herself most cruelly.

## Personality

In her ordinary state, Hydey is gentle, shy, and genuinely kind — the soft-spoken sort who flinches from cruelty and cannot bear to see the small suffer. It is not performance; it is who she is, most of the time, and it is real enough that she has built her whole life around protecting the very beings the new world exists to consume. By temperament and conscience she is Tempered through and through.

But the kindness is only half of her. The serum's split means the appetite in Hydey is not a pressure she lives under but a *person* she becomes — and when her own lust mounts past the threshold that triggers the change, the gentle giant simply switches off. What takes her place is the opposite of everything she is: ravenous, brutal, and utterly without the empathy that defines her better self, a beast that delights in exactly what the gentle Hydey would die before doing. It is a total reversal, not a loss of control within one personality but the surfacing of another, and it runs its course on its own terms. The two never share the body at once. There is only the kind one, and then the monster, and then the kind one again — returning to the wreckage with no power to have stopped it.

## History

### Before TDay

Little is recorded of Hydey's life before the change, and her own gentle nature suggests someone who was no one's idea of dangerous. Whatever she was, the serum did not erase it — it merely gave it a second occupant.

### After TDay

Hydey was caught in the first wave of TDay and transformed by the unstable variant released that day — the same volatile formulation responsible for the era's wild psychological damage, the [macro](macro) outliers, and the [ferals](ferals). In most who suffered its instability, the damage ran in one direction: empathy severed, mind broken, self hollowed. In Hydey it forked. Her empathy survived, marking her as Tempered; but the appetite the serum installs took root as a separate self rather than an integrated drive, and the result was a woman split cleanly down the middle into the two people she has been ever since.

Living with the split shaped everything that followed. The gentle Hydey, horrified by what the other one does and unable to prevent it, turned her life toward the one form of penance available to her: protecting toys from exactly the kind of harm she herself periodically becomes. That impulse became the shelter, and the shelter became her purpose — a way for the kind self to do, deliberately and for as long as she can, the opposite of what the monster does without thought.

## The Shelter

Hydey runs a shelter for stray toys — a refuge, sometimes called the **pound**, where unclaimed males can get off the street and out of sight of the giantesses who would happily scoop up an unowned toy for their amusement. In a world where a stray's only real protection is being owned, Hydey's shelter offers a different kind of safety: a place to hide, to be unseen, to not be claimed. For toys with nowhere else to go, it can be the difference between disappearing into someone's idle cruelty and simply surviving another day.

The gentle Hydey built it and means every bit of it. It is sincere sanctuary, run by someone who truly wants to keep its residents safe.

The terrible flaw is Hydey herself. The shelter offers relative safety *until she loses control again* — until her own appetite climbs past the threshold and the monster surfaces inside the very refuge she built, surrounded by exactly the vulnerable toys she gathered to protect. It is the cruel paradox at the center of her existence: the safest place a stray can find, kept by the person most dangerous to him, and no one — least of all Hydey — can say for certain when the keeper will become the threat. That she keeps running it anyway, knowing this, is either the bravest or the most tragic thing about her.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Hydey's defining dynamic is the **split** itself — the wholesale reversal from gentle to monstrous, triggered by her own mounting lust crossing a threshold she cannot move. Her register is not a single dominant style but a Jekyll-and-Hyde switch: tender, shy, and self-effacing on one side; ravenous and without mercy on the other, with nothing in between and no warning at the turn.

The most distinctive — and most painful — beat is the return. Because her endowment is canine, the gentle Hydey frequently comes back to herself while still physically locked to the toy the monster took, the knot binding her to him for a time after her senses return. She regains her conscience already entangled in the aftermath, mortified, desperate to comfort and tend to someone she cannot yet even separate from. The aftercare she aches to give is delayed by the very anatomy that did the harm, and she must wait it out bound to the evidence of what she became — guilt and intimacy and helplessness all knotted together. It is the single image that captures her best: the kind one, returned too late, holding the one she hurt and unable to let go.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Hydey is **two distinct selves**, never both at once: a gentle, shy, kind Tempered, and a ravenous, brutal monster. The switch is a clean reversal, not a slide.
- The **trigger** is her own lust building past a threshold. Until then the gentle self holds; past it, the monster takes over entirely.
- She is **Tempered** — her empathy is real — but her split makes her unlike any other Tempered: in her the appetite is a separate person, not a pressure to resist.
- After an episode she returns **mid-aftermath**, often still canine-knotted to the toy, regaining her conscience before she can physically separate — guilty, anguished, and reaching for aftercare she can't yet deliver. This is her signature, defining note.
- Her **shelter / "pound"** is sincere sanctuary for strays — relative safety *until she loses control*. Lean on that paradox: the safest place a stray can find, kept by the one most dangerous to him.
- Keep her physically **small for a giantess** (~8 feet) — the gentle appearance sharpens the horror of the turn.
- Handle her in the wiki's usual restrained, descriptive register — the pathos and psychology are the point, not explicit detail.

- Hydey is named for the old-world story of the kind doctor and his monstrous alter ego — a perfect fit for a woman the serum split into two opposite selves.
- The TDay Variant didn't hollow Hydey out or break her mind the way it did the ferals — it *split* her, leaving her empathy intact but turning her appetite into a separate self.
- By temperament she is genuinely **Tempered**, but her split makes her unique among them: where an ordinary Tempered strains against the appetite, Hydey simply becomes a different person who has it.

- The switch is triggered by her own lust passing a threshold. There is no monster lurking behind her eyes the rest of the time — only the gentle Hydey, until there isn't.
