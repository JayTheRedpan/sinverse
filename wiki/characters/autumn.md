## Overview

Autumn is a Clydesdale mare and one of the new world's stockraisers. Where most of the transformed simply take what they want and discard the rest, Autumn runs a working **breeding farm** — a ranch out in the country where men are kept, trained, and bred like livestock, each one a "pet" or a head of "stock" in her drawling estimation. She is a rancher to the bone: proud of her animals, patient with her training, and possessed of an unusual virtue among the apotheosis girls — restraint. Autumn keeps her stock alive because good stock is worth keeping, and she has open contempt for those who break their toys too fast to enjoy them.

At a little over twenty-two feet she's modest by the standards of the towering giantesses, but on her own land, surrounded by pens of plugged and bridled ponies, Autumn is unquestionably the master of all she surveys.

## Appearance

Autumn is a Clydesdale with a cream-colored coat and a flowing ivory mane, tail, and hair, framing a long equine face and bright turquoise eyes that can shift from dangerous to beautiful in a blink. She has the heavy, powerful build of a draft horse scaled up to roughly twenty-two feet — broad-shouldered, thick-armed, wide-hipped, every inch of her built like she could haul a plow and then some. She favors practical farm wear, most often a flannel shirt straining to contain her, and she carries herself with the easy, grounded confidence of someone who works with her hands.

The serum left her with the equine endowment to match her species — an enormous stallion's length that features heavily in the "breeding" she's named her trade for.

## Personality

Autumn is folksy, gruff, and drawling — a country rancher in both manner and speech, equal parts warm and casually brutal. She talks to her stock the way a stern farmhand talks to animals: scolding, praising, sizing them up by their build and their "padding," and dispensing discipline with an even hand. There's a genuine pride in her work; she takes real satisfaction in a well-trained pet and a well-run farm, and she lavishes a rough sort of nurturing on the stock she values, complete with special diets and patient conditioning.

Her defining trait is **restraint** — a thing in short supply among the transformed. Autumn deliberately holds back the urge to simply crush and consume the tiny men around her, because she understands that good stock is an investment and momentary pleasure is a waste of it. "Quality over quantity" is practically her creed. She'll indulge the urge on a "squirmer" — a runt not worth keeping — but a prize pet is too valuable to ruin. This makes her, paradoxically, one of the more survivable predators in the setting for a toy lucky enough to be deemed worth keeping, and one of the most permanently enslaving: Autumn doesn't kill her best stock, she *keeps* them.

## History

### Before TDay

Little is recorded of Autumn's life before the world changed, though her temperament and trade suggest rural, working-class roots — someone already comfortable with animals, land, and the unsentimental logic of raising livestock.

### After TDay

When the serum remade the world, Autumn took to the new order with the practical mindset of a rancher rather than the abandon of a predator. Rather than roam and hunt, she established a **breeding farm** — a ranch where men are kept as "stock," housed in pens, broken to the saddle, and trained into compliant "ponies" and "cock sleeves." She bridles and plugs her pets, gives the favored ones names — Rain, Bent, Crew, White among them — and works them on a regimen of conditioning, discipline, and a special diet, treating the whole enterprise as serious animal husbandry.

She sources fresh stock through a business arrangement with **Sin**, purchasing transformed-world "product" to train on her farm — and she's not shy about critiquing the matriarch's methods, needling Sin for breaking her own toys too quickly. The two are associates of long standing, trading stock and barbs in equal measure, and Autumn attends the gatherings of the new world's elite with a few of her best ponies in tow.

## As a Rancher

Autumn's whole identity is built around the farm. She runs it like a true stockraiser: evaluating new arrivals by their build and temperament, culling the runts, and investing months of patient training into the ones with promise. Her pets are kept bridled, plugged, and tailed, conditioned into specific roles, and fed and disciplined on a strict routine. She takes pride in producing "stock" of a quality she insists is better than anyone else's, and she measures her success not in how many toys she's used up but in how many fine, well-trained pets she's built and kept.

The flip side of that pride is her restraint: where another giantess might crush a pet on a whim, Autumn consciously stays her hand, reminding herself not to waste good stock. The runts and the unsalvageable — the "squirmers" — are another matter, dispatched casually, sometimes simply eaten, with none of the care she reserves for her prize animals.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Autumn's tastes run entirely to the **rancher's fantasy**: the training, breaking, and keeping of men as livestock. Her great satisfaction comes from **pony play and breeding dynamics** — bridling, plugging, and tailing her pets, conditioning them into living "cock sleeves," and the slow, proprietary work of turning a fresh, struggling arrival into a well-trained piece of stock. She savors discipline and obedience above all: a pet who holds still for inspection, who's learned to take her without complaint, who performs his role well, earns her rough, genuine approval — and she takes real pleasure in the husbandry of it, the diet and the drilling and the gradual reshaping of a body to her use. Her equine endowment is the centerpiece of her "breeding," and her best-trained ponies are kept precisely because they've been made to accommodate it. Underlying all of it is her trademark restraint: the eroticism for Autumn is in *keeping* and *perfecting* her stock, not destroying it — though the runts and squirmers she has no use for are crushed or swallowed without a second thought, a casual cruelty that throws the care she shows her prize pets into sharp relief.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Autumn's defining concept is the **breeding farm** — she keeps men as trained "stock"/"ponies," not casual prey. Lean on the rancher framing: husbandry, training, bridles, pens.
- Her signature virtue is **restraint** — "quality over quantity." She deliberately doesn't waste good stock, which makes her permanently enslaving rather than quick to kill. The casual disposal of "squirmers" is the contrast that defines it.
- She's **folksy and drawling** — write her with a country-rancher voice, gruff but capable of rough warmth toward favored pets.
- Her relationship with **Sin** is a **business association** — she buys stock from Sin and freely criticizes her for breaking toys too fast. They are peers and associates, not close friends.
- Named pets established in canon: **Rain, Bent, Crew, White**. She has **no faction**.
