## Overview

Riley is a raccoon-red panda hybrid, born into the world after [TDay](tday) and raised far from the parents who made her. She is the child of [Jay](jay) and [Libby](libby) — though she was not brought up by either of them, and would not thank you for the connection. Riley carries a deep, smouldering hatred for her red panda parent, a loathing fed partly by her own disgust at being tied by blood to a *toy*, and partly by the vicious giantess who raised her in their place.

What put Riley in that giantess's care rather than her parents' has never been made clear, and was likely not wholly anyone's choice. What's certain is the result: a young giantess with a chip the size of her resentment, a hard goth shell, and a habit of taking out on other red panda toys the fury she holds for the one who bore her. She is still finding her place in the new world, and not much else about her is known for sure.

## Appearance

Riley reads as almost fully red panda at a glance, with the species' familiar colouring and markings — but her raccoon heritage surfaces at the edges. The fur of her extremities, her arms and her legs, fades from the shared white into a grey, raccoon-like tone, and her tail tells the same mixed story: long like a red panda's, but banded in the grey rings of a raccoon's. Her eyes are a yellow-gold.

Everything else about her presentation is a deliberate construction. Riley embraces the goth look completely: pitch-black medium-length hair falling down over her eyes, heavy black eyeliner, black lipstick, and extra black makeup worked into her red panda markings to darken and sharpen them into something harder. She wears triple piercings in her left ear and a double eyebrow piercing on the left, a black choker at her throat, and traditional goth attire — fishnets, deliberately undersized ill-fitting black skirts. Her equine length is pierced along its length, something she inherited from neither parent and chose for herself. Her body carries multiple tattoos, including the word **"Daddy"** across her chest, alongside others that speak, none too subtly, to her hatred of her red panda parent.

## Personality

Riley is hard, guarded, and angry, with the resentful edge of someone who has never felt she belonged anywhere. The goth presentation is armour as much as aesthetic — a deliberate, spiky shell she's built around herself while she works out who she is and where she fits in a world that handed her a difficult start.

The defining feature of her interior life is the hatred. Riley despises her red panda parent with a depth that has become load-bearing to her whole identity, and it springs from two sources twined together. The first is her own revulsion at the connection itself: to be the blood child of a *toy*, in a world that treats toys as livestock, is to Riley a kind of contamination, a shameful thing to be scoured away. The second is the influence of the giantess who raised her — affectionately called her **"aunt"** — who stoked that ember into a steady fire, feeding Riley's disgust until it became a settled, cultivated hatred rather than a child's confusion.

That hatred doesn't stay abstract. Unable to reach the parent she actually loathes, Riley turns it outward onto other red panda toys, using them as stand-ins — venting on them the fury she holds for the one who bore her. It is a displaced, restless cruelty, and it hints at how unresolved the whole knot inside her remains. For all her hard edges, Riley is still young, still searching, and still defined more by what she's reacting against than by anything she's chosen for herself.

## History

### Before TDay

Riley has no before. She was born into the world after TDay, a child of the new order who never knew the world that came before it.

### After TDay

Riley is the child of Jay and Libby — born, as the new world's children are, through SinCorp's reproductive science, since [the transformed](the-transformed) women have no womb to carry a child of their own. By rights she might have grown up in the rare warmth of her parents' bond. She did not.

How Riley came to be raised by another instead has never been established, and was likely not wholly within Jay or Libby's power to decide. What's known is who took that place: a sabertooth giantess of vicious reputation, who took a particular liking to the young Riley and raised her as her own. Riley came to call her **"aunt,"** with what genuine affection the word carries — and it was this aunt who shaped the angry, hardened young giantess Riley became, stoking her disgust at her toy parentage into the deep-seated hatred she carries now.

Riley knows enough of Jay to despise him, and that loathing has grown into something she acts on, displacing it onto other red panda toys who serve as stand-ins for the parent she can't confront. What she knows or feels about Libby is far less clear — an unresolved thread in a life still full of them. Riley is, by every sign, still trying to find her place in the world, and much of her story remains unwritten.

## As a Stray Cruelty

Riley's hatred has a shape, and the shape is other red panda toys. Denied the chance to direct her fury at the parent who actually inspires it, she finds toys of her parent's species and makes them carry it for him — taking out on these stand-ins the resentment, disgust, and rage that properly belong to a confrontation she's never had. It is cruelty as displacement, less a cold appetite than a wound expressing itself, and it marks her as a giantess whose worst impulses are tangled up with her unhealed history rather than springing from simple sadism.

Beyond that, Riley's place in the world is genuinely unsettled. She holds no title, claims no faction, and serves no one — a hard-shelled young giantess still circling the question of who she is, shaped so far more by her aunt and her grievances than by any path she's chosen.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Riley's register is shaped by her hatred and her hard, deliberate self-presentation. Her cruelty toward toys — and especially toward red panda toys, who stand in for the parent she despises — carries a personal, displaced charge that sets it apart from simpler appetites: there is grievance in it, a wound being worked out on a body that can't answer for it. The **"Daddy"** tattooed across her chest and her self-chosen piercings speak to a dominance she wears as armour and identity both, hard-edged and performative, of a piece with the goth shell she's built around a still-unsettled interior.

- She **knows of Jay** enough to hate him; her **knowledge and feelings about Libby are deliberately unclear.** Don't resolve the Libby thread.
- Her cruelty is **displaced and wound-driven**, not cold sadism — grievance acting itself out. She's young and still finding her place.
- Her look is hard **goth**: pitch-black hair over her eyes, heavy black makeup darkening her red panda markings, triple ear / double eyebrow piercings (left side), black choker, fishnets, undersized black skirts. - Much about Riley is **deliberately unknown** — lean into the open questions rather than filling them in.
