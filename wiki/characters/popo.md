## Overview

Popo is one half of the inseparable pair behind one of the new world's toy shops — the bright, bouncing, insatiable half. A brown bunny with a giggle never far from her lips, she works as the [huntress](the-paws) of the operation she shares with [Juxta](juxta), ranging out to catch the fresh toys their shop takes in, trains, and sells. The two are so rarely apart, and so plainly devoted to one another, that they are widely called *sisters* — though the word is affection rather than fact; they share no blood, only a partnership and a closeness that runs as deep as any family.

Popo is bubbly, excitable, and almost childishly delighted by the world she finds herself in. She is also a near-total slave to her own lust, to a degree that can leave her giddy and unfocused, and it falls to the steadier Juxta to keep her pointed in the right direction. In the old world, Popo's appetites would have been a problem she struggled against. In this one, they are simply her bliss — and the engine that makes her so very good at her work.

## Appearance

Popo is a brown bunny with short brown hair and bright green eyes, her look defined by a sweep of **red-dyed tips** in her bangs that hang down over her face. She stands nine and a half feet — modest by the standards of the towering [Transformed](the-transformed), but enormous beside the toys she hunts and adores. Her whole bearing is energy barely contained: bouncing, fidgeting, lit up with an excitement she makes no effort to hide.

The serum left her endowed in a humanoid form. On Popo the effect reads less as menace than as one more outlet for an appetite she is forever, happily, at the mercy of.

## Personality

Popo is, in a word, *exuberant.* She is giggly and bouncy and over-excitable, forever delighted by something, the kind of relentless cheer that would be exhausting if it weren't so genuine. She loves her work, loves her toys, loves her life in the new world — and would be the picture of harmless, sunny enthusiasm if not for the appetite running underneath all of it.

Because beneath the bubbliness, Popo is a slave to her lust. It is not an act and not an occasional thing; it is the central fact of her, an insatiable drive so strong it can, in her own description, "melt her brain" — leaving her giddy, scattered, and incapable of focusing on anything but the want. This is precisely why she and Juxta work: where Popo's appetite carries her away, Juxta hauls her back, keeping her on task and on schedule when her own head has dissolved into need. Left to herself, Popo would simply chase the next satisfaction until she forgot she had a shop to stock.

What makes her unsettling — far more so than her stern partner — is the total disconnect between her sweetness and what that sweetness does. Popo does not mean to be cruel. She giggles, she coos, she adores the little things she catches, and she has seemingly no awareness at all of how brutal her affection is at her scale and under her appetite. She is delighted while a toy is terrified, and cannot understand why he isn't delighted too.

## History

### Before TDay

Little is recorded of Popo's life before the change, and she is far too occupied with the present to dwell on it. What carried across the transformation was the appetite — the same insatiable drive, given a body and a world that could finally indulge it without limit.

### After TDay

For Popo, the new world is a personal paradise. Where the old one would have made her appetites a source of shame or struggle — the sort of bottomless craving a person hides — [TDay](tday) rewrote the order so that her lust was no longer a flaw but an asset, and her giddy enthusiasm for indulging it simply made her exceptional at the work the new world had on offer. She fell in with Juxta, and the two built their shop together: Popo to bring the toys in, Juxta to make something sellable of them. It is a partnership that has lasted because it is also a friendship, two halves of a whole who are seldom seen apart and are taken, by nearly everyone, for sisters.

## As a Huntress

Popo's role in the partnership is the hunt — ranging out to catch the toys the shop will train and sell. She is unnervingly well-suited to it, and the reason is exactly the appetite that makes her such a handful everywhere else. Popo *enjoys* the chase, openly and without a flicker of the predator's seriousness; she giggles while her quarry flees in terror, genuinely delighted by the game, her bottomless lust turning the hunt into the most fun she can imagine. A predator who hates her prey is frightening. A predator who is having the time of her life is something worse.

She is not a professional of the [Paws](the-paws) in the organised, code-bound sense — she hunts for her own shop, not the open trade — but she ranges with the same purpose and brings back the same prize: fresh toys, caught by a bright-eyed bunny who cooed at them the whole way in.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Popo's defining trait is **insatiable lust**, played as bliss rather than malice. Her register is the giddy, overwhelming affection of someone who simply cannot get enough and cannot conceive that the object of it might not feel the same — a relentless, bubbly appetite that runs a toy ragged precisely because she is enjoying herself far too much to notice his limits. The horror, where there is horror, comes entirely from the gap between her sunny adoration and the brutality of it at her scale: Popo is never *trying* to be cruel, which is exactly what makes her the one the shop's toys learn to dread most.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Popo is **bubbly, giggly, and exuberant** — relentlessly cheerful, easily excited, never far from a giggle.
- Her core trait is **bottomless lust** that can "melt her brain," leaving her scattered and unfocused. She needs **Juxta** to keep her on task.
- She is the **scarier of the two**, despite Juxta's sternness — not from cruelty but from a complete lack of awareness of how brutal her giddy affection is. Write the disconnect: delighted while her toy is terrified.
- As a **huntress**, she giggles through the chase and enjoys it openly. A predator having fun, not a grim professional.
- She is not literally Juxta's sister — they're inseparable partners called sisters out of affection.
- Her look: brown bunny, short brown hair, green eyes, **red-dyed bang tips** over her face.

## Did You Know

- Popo and [Juxta](juxta) were the very first two characters ever created in the setting.
- Popo, Juxta, and [Sin](sin) were all named after the theme of **juxtaposition** — a core principle of the setting, written right into its founding trio.
- Popo and Juxta are called sisters, but the term is pure affection — they share no blood, only an inseparable partnership.
- Despite Juxta being the stern one, it is bubbly Popo the shop's toys learn to fear most — her bottomless lust and obliviousness to her own brutality make her affection more dangerous than any sternness.
- Popo giggles while her quarry flees. Her insatiable appetite makes the hunt a delight to her, which makes her uniquely unsettling at it.
- Her lust can, by her own account, "melt her brain" — which is much of why she needs Juxta to keep the partnership pointed in the right direction.
