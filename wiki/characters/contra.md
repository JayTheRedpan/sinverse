## Overview

Contra Tyung is a house cat and, in the new world, the apex of the very kind of place that once preyed on her. Before TDay she was a secretary — meek, skittish, and ground down by a middle-manager boss who dictated a demeaning "uniform" for her and treated her as office decoration. After TDay she did not flee the corporate world; she inherited it. The firm that once leered at her now answers to her, and the men who ran it occupy a very different place in the hierarchy than they used to.

She is a study in cold inversion. Where she was once timid prey, she is now the unbothered predator at the top of the org chart — running her corner of the post-TDay economy with the same casual cruelty that was once aimed at her, and keeping the men beneath her exactly as disposable as they once considered her.

## Appearance

Contra is a house cat built to a commanding scale — roughly eighteen feet of poised, deliberate menace. Her coat is tan and brown with a soft cream underbelly, set off by sharp green eyes and short red hair kept in a neat, businesslike cut. Pre-TDay she carried herself like a doe among wolves: tense shoulders, a stiff tail, the body language of someone perpetually braced for the next unwanted comment. The transformation kept the elegance and stripped out the fear. She favors sharp, corporate presentation — the polished, put-together look of an executive who has never once doubted she belongs at the head of the table.

The serum left her endowed in place of the genitalia it took, a humanoid length she wields as a matter of dominance over the toys in her keeping rather than display.

## Personality

Contra's defining trait is the completeness of her reversal. The old Contra flinched, apologized, and shrank — she absorbed harassment because talking back would have cost her the job, and her fear was so habitual it read as part of her posture. The new Contra has buried that woman entirely. What replaced her is composed, patient, and glacially cruel: an executive predator who runs her domain by an unsentimental logic in which men are plentiful, disposable, and valued only by what they can do for her.

She is territorial in the way the transformed tend to be — possessive of her space, her status, and her toys — and she enforces that territory without raising her voice. She doesn't rage; she decides. A man who fails her isn't screamed at, he's reassigned to a worse fate with a bored sigh and a flick of the wrist, and she takes a flat, proprietary satisfaction in how thoroughly the order of things has flipped in her favor. The skittish secretary is gone. In her place is someone who learned exactly how power works by being on the wrong end of it, and now applies the lesson without a flicker of remorse.

## History

### Before TDay

Contra worked as a secretary at a corporate firm under a lecherous middle-manager who treated her as eye candy and a personal project. He personally "recommended" the revealing uniform she was made to wear, peppered her with unwanted advances dressed up as old-fashioned charm, and counted on her timidity to keep her compliant. She endured it the way she endured everything — head down, grin too tight, arms wrapped around whatever she was carrying like a shield — because the alternative was losing her livelihood. She was, by every measure of that office, prey.

### After TDay

When the transformations came, the balance of that office didn't just shift — it shattered and re-formed with Contra at the top. Improbably, firms like hers survived into the new world, kept running by the transformed women who now staffed and ran them, with men retained as labor and as "toys" held in check by the simple fact that a drained, satisfied woman was a manageable one.

Contra rose to command her firm completely. The boss who once dictated her uniform and the colleagues who once joined in are now far below her in a hierarchy she sets, and she runs it as a cold, territorial enterprise: men kept on as pets and disposable staff, expected to perform without complaint, disciplined without mercy when they fail. She maintains the trappings of a functioning workplace — order, procedure, the appearance of professionalism — over a reality in which the men who work there serve entirely at her pleasure and survive entirely at her discretion.

## As an Executive Predator

Contra runs her corner of the post-TDay corporate world like a true matriarch of industry — calm, procedural, and utterly without mercy. She keeps male toys both for use and as a labor pool, and she governs them through a chillingly bureaucratic cruelty: failure is "disciplined," underperformers are "reassigned," and the worst fates are handed down as if they were routine HR decisions. The horror of her domain is precisely how mundane she makes it sound. She rarely needs force; the structure does the work, and her toys learn to dread a bored, polite sentence far more than a raised hand.

She takes a possessive, proprietary pleasure in her staff and her status, and the men who fail to be useful discover exactly how disposable they are in her ledger.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Contra's tastes are an extension of her cold corporate cruelty: **emasculation and sissification**. Having spent her old life made small and decorative by men, she takes a calculated, proprietary pleasure in doing the same to them — and for Contra the appeal lives almost entirely in the *embarrassment*. It isn't enough to overpower a man; she wants to humiliate him out of his masculinity piece by piece and make him feel every degrading step of it. She dresses her toys in things chosen specifically to mortify them, drills them on mincing little behaviors and demure manners, assigns them simpering new names, and makes them perform their own feminization aloud — curtsying, asking permission, thanking her for each fresh indignity — in front of an audience whenever she can arrange one, because a man's shame is sharpest when it's witnessed. She runs it all with the flat, bored detachment of a manager conducting a performance review, which only deepens the degradation: there's no passion in it for him to rage against, just the unbearable sense of being calmly, methodically remade into something he's been taught to find shameful. The blush, the stammered compliance, the visible wish to sink through the floor — that mortification is the entire point for Contra, far more than any physical act. She savors the breaking of a man's pride one humiliating requirement at a time, taking the exact dynamic once used to demean her and turning it back on the men responsible.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Contra's engine is **total inversion** — meek, harassed secretary to cold corporate apex. Keep both halves in view; her cruelty is the fear she used to feel turned outward.
- Her register is **cold and procedural**, not hot-blooded. She decides rather than rages; the bureaucratic framing of her cruelty is the point.
- Her **former boss is backstory only and unnamed** — reference the dynamic, not the individual, unless coordinated.
- She is **territorial and possessive** of her domain and her toys, consistent with the transformed.
- She has **no faction** — her allegiance is to her own enterprise and standing.
