## Overview

Usiku is a [macro](macro)-scale [Transformed](the-transformed) and a former fashion designer whose art survived her transformation — and curdled into something monstrous. A towering jackal standing nearly sixty-seven feet, she was carried by the unstable [TDay](tday) Variant of the [Apotheosis Serum](apotheosis-serum) to a scale that brought the mental damage so often paired with it. In Usiku, that damage did not erase the designer she had been; it warped her. She remains, in her own mind, an artist devoted to beauty — but she has come to believe that [toys](toys) are the ultimate medium, and she adorns her own body with them as living jewellery, arranging and displaying them upon herself in various ways as the centrepieces of an aesthetic only she fully understands.

## Appearance

Usiku is a jackal of roughly sixty-seven feet — deep into macro scale, a creature of the open [Wilds](the-wilds) rather than any city. The serum left her endowed in an equine form. Her presentation is, by her own design, a constant work of art: she decorates herself elaborately, and the most distinctive elements of her adornment are alive.

## Personality

What is established of Usiku is her fixation. The TDay Variant's instability damaged her mind without destroying the designer's sensibility she carried from the old world — and the result is an artist's obsession turned on living bodies. She regards toys as the supreme material for her craft, more expressive than any fabric or stone, and she works them into her own presentation as living jewellery: ornaments, accents, and centrepieces affixed to and displayed upon her enormous frame. To Usiku this is high art and self-expression of the most refined kind. That her medium is sentient is, to her, precisely what makes it sublime.

## History

### Before TDay

Usiku was a fashion designer before the change — an artist by trade, with an eye and an obsession for beauty that the transformation would carry across intact.

### After TDay

Usiku was transformed by the TDay Variant and grew well past the threshold at which the formulation's instability brings cognitive damage. Rather than destroying her, the damage warped what she already was: the designer remained, but her sense of medium slipped from cloth to flesh, and she came to see toys as the ultimate material for her art. She has pursued that vision ever since, at macro scale, out where her size and her appetites have room.
