## Overview

Becka is a lioness and one half of an inseparable pair of twin sisters, the other being her sister Bella. Where most of [the transformed](the-transformed) hunt alone, Becka and Bella hunt together — and, more unusually, they *share*. The twins are defined by their bond: a giggling, bickering, fiercely close partnership that turns even their cruelty into a joint activity. To a [toy](toys), that closeness is no comfort. Two apex predators who finish each other's sentences and cooperate by instinct are far harder to survive than one.

Becka is the marginally cooler-headed half of the pair — the one who comes up with the "ideas," delivers the cruelest suggestions in an even, almost thoughtful tone, and lets her more excitable sister supply the squealing enthusiasm. She is every bit as monstrous; she's just quieter about it.

## Appearance

Becka is a largely ordinary anthro lioness scaled up to roughly eighteen feet — tawny, athletic, and built with the easy muscle of a big cat. She has long brownish hair, which she wears pulled back in a ponytail; this is, along with her sister's pigtails, one of the only reliable ways to tell the two of them apart, as they are otherwise identical. The simpler, sleeker ponytail fits her temperament: a touch more composed than her twin, a little more deliberate.

The serum left her endowed in place of the genitalia it took — a humanoid length, identical to her twin's, which the two of them are equally happy to put to use on a shared toy.

## Personality

Becka is the dry, idea-having twin — calmer than Bella on the surface, with a habit of voicing the most disturbing suggestions in a flat, matter-of-fact tone that makes them land harder for the contrast. She is the one who decides what to do with a toy while Bella cheers; she names things, frames things, and turns the sisters' fun into something with a sick little structure to it. Beneath the composure she is exactly as indifferent to her victims as her sister — she simply expresses it as wry amusement rather than giddy excitement.

Her defining relationship is with Bella. The twins squabble constantly — over toys, over whose idea was better, over nothing — but it never threatens their bond; bickering is just how they talk to each other, and it resolves the instant something more fun presents itself. Becka tends to "win" these exchanges by simply proposing something more interesting, and Bella's delight at her sister's ideas is one of the few things that reliably ends a quarrel. Left to her own devices Becka might fight a sister tooth and claw for a prize, but the two of them long ago discovered they'd rather share than be apart, and that discovery is the engine of who they are.

## History

### Before TDay

Becka and Bella were the step-daughters of a wealthy human man — daughters of the lioness he'd married, and step-sisters to his human son, with whom they shared a home but no blood and no species. They grew up in money that wasn't theirs, looked down on by a step-brother who considered humans his betters and never let them forget it.

### TDay

When the transformations swept the world, the twins changed alongside every other woman — their family's wealthy estate collapsing around them as they outgrew it. In the chaos of that first day, their step-brother fled, abandoning them and driving off without looking back. The twins, newly enormous and newly hungry, were not the kind to simply let that go.

When they eventually ran their fugitive step-brother to ground at a remote family property, he became their **first toy** — and the experience cemented the dynamic that would define them. Rather than fight over him as two predators might be expected to, Becka and Bella discovered they enjoyed sharing far more, working in tandem on a single victim with the eerie coordination of twins. It was Becka who decided, at the end, that the broken thing they were left with was no longer a brother but a pet — and gave it the demeaning new name to match. They kept him: the first of many toys to learn that being caught by one of the sisters meant being caught by both.

### After TDay

Becka and Bella remain inseparable, roaming the new world as a unit and keeping toys between them rather than apart. They have no faction and no cause beyond each other; their world is small, self-contained, and centered entirely on the two of them and whatever plaything currently holds their attention.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Becka's defining kink — shared completely with her sister — is exactly that: **sharing a single toy**. The twins take a particular delight in using one victim together, often after squabbling over who gets to do what, and the act becomes a kind of collaborative performance between them. A toy caught by Becka and Bella is used from both ends at once, pinned between two coordinated predators who treat it as the shared centerpiece of their fun and barely seem to notice its suffering past how it serves their game. It is an experience few toys can physically endure — two apex giants, perfectly in sync, with none of the distraction or restraint that a single partner might occasionally show. Becka tends to be the one steering: dreaming up what they'll do, narrating the toy's fate in her dry, unbothered way, and assigning the humiliating names and roles that turn a victim into a "pet." For Becka the appeal is as much about doing it *with Bella* as it is about the toy; the shared cruelty is a bonding ritual, and the plaything is mostly the thing they bond over.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Becka is almost always written **alongside Bella** — the twins are a unit, and their shared-toy dynamic is the whole point. A Becka scene without her sister should be the rare exception.
- She is the **dry, idea-having** half to Bella's bubbly one — she proposes the cruelties in a flat, matter-of-fact register and tends to name and frame things.
- Their **bickering is affectionate** and never a real threat to their bond — it's how they relate, and it resolves instantly.
- The two are physically **identical except for hair** — Becka's ponytail, Bella's pigtails.
- They have **no faction** — their allegiance is to each other.
