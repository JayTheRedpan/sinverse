## Overview

Xena is a macro-scale Transformed who rules a stretch of the [Wilds](the-wilds) as, in her own mind, a warlord. A towering T-Rex standing nearly fifty feet, she was carried by the unstable TDay Variant of the [Apotheosis Serum](apotheosis-serum) up to a scale that brought the mental damage so often paired with it — and in Xena, that damage took the shape of a grand, fixed delusion. She is convinced she is a conqueror, that the territory she occupies is a domain she won and holds, and that she reigns over it by right. Whether anyone else recognises her "rule" is, to Xena, beside the point. She is certain of it, and she rules accordingly.

*This profile is partially documented. The details of Xena's character beyond her defining delusion are still being developed.*

## Appearance

Xena is a T-Rex of roughly forty-nine feet — well into macro scale, far too large for any mega-city, a living siege engine of a creature suited only to the open Wilds. The serum left her endowed in an equine form, mismatched to her saurian frame in the way the new world's hybrids so often are.

## Personality

What is established of Xena is her delusion. The TDay Variant's instability damaged her mind in a particular direction: she believes herself a warlord, a ruler of conquered ground, and she carries herself with the absolute conviction of one. She holds her patch of the Wilds as a sovereign holds a kingdom — territory, dominion, and the certainty of her own authority over it — untroubled by whether the wider world has ever heard her name.

### After TDay

Xena was transformed by the TDay Variant and grew well past the threshold at which the formulation's instability brings cognitive damage. Where some macros lost their minds entirely to that damage, Xena's broke into a single organising delusion — the conviction of conquest and rule that defines her — and she carried it out into the Wilds, where she has claimed her "domain" ever since.
