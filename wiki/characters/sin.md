## Overview

Sin is the titular figure of the Sinverse and the progenitor of the world as it now exists. A black bunny of genius-level intellect, she discovered the Apotheosis Serum, tested it on herself, and orchestrated the event known as TDay — the day the world was remade in her image. She is the founder of SinCorp and the Matriarch of Angora, the unofficial capital of the post-TDay world.

She stands 13 feet tall. To the giantesses who rule the mega-cities she commands respect; to the toys who live beneath them, she is something closer to a god — and a boogeyman.

## Appearance

Sin is a black bunny, her fur dark and her hair deeper still — a black so complete it seems to drink the light. A single golden streak runs through her bangs. It appears entirely natural, which surprises those who meet her: Sin is not the type to dye her hair or spare a thought for vanity.

Her transformation reshaped her into more than a giantess. She bears equine genitalia — a change many believe she chose deliberately, engineering herself to maximise her capacity to dominate and ruin a toy. Her original cottontail was likewise replaced by a long, horse-like tail in the same black as her hair, which she typically keeps bound in a neat braid to keep it out of her way while she works.

## Personality

Sin possesses a super-genius intellect and the cold focus to match it. She is far more interested in her next creation than in anything as tedious as ruling, and she carries herself with the detachment of someone for whom most other people — giantess or toy — are simply not relevant to the problem in front of her.

What is not detached is her contempt for men. The reason has never been confirmed. It is insinuated that she was abused, or simply looked down upon for her size and her gender in the world before — and that a mind as formidable as hers, made to feel *small*, curdled that humiliation into something vast and patient. Whatever its origin, she built an entire civilization to express it.

She is unbothered by the suffering she presides over. More than unbothered — she cultivates it. Sin takes special, deliberate care to maintain a society in which toys are kept and traded as, in her own words, "sexual livestock."

## History

### Before TDay

Before TDay, Sin was a researcher at a bio-sciences and genetics laboratory. It was there, working with a mind that outstripped everyone around her, that she synthesised the first working batch of the serum she would come to call Apotheosis — and administered it to herself, the first test subject in history. What walked out of that lab was no longer what had walked in.

She did not announce herself. She worked from the shadows, founding her own research operation — SinCorp — where she perfected the serum and laid the groundwork for TDay. She funded the work through government and military contracts, selling early precursors to what would later become toy-durability treatments under a more palatable name: *soldier durability treatments.* The military paid for the very science that would one day be used to keep their kind alive under the women who replaced them.

A number of the men in Sin's life did not survive her ascent. Her old boss, for one, simply disappeared — as did others who had known her before. No bodies, no explanations. By the time anyone thought to wonder, the world had bigger changes to reckon with.

### After TDay

Once the Chaos subsided and the mega-cities reformed, SinCorp reformed with them — every mega-city now hosts its own SinCorp laboratory, each pursuing its own branch of devious and frequently unsettling research at any given time. Sin sits at the centre of it all as the Matriarch of Angora, the unofficial capital.

She is largely uninterested in the responsibilities of running a city. Governance bores her; she would far rather be at work on her next creation. What she does not neglect is the architecture of the society itself — she continues, personally and deliberately, to ensure the world remains one in which toys are treated as sexual livestock. The cruelty of the world is not an accident she tolerates. It is a design she maintains.

Most toys who are taken to meet Sin are never seen in the light of day again.

## As a Domme

Where the other dominant women of the world are meant to instill fear, Sin is meant to *be* fear — the ultimate boogeyman of the setting. The mere prospect of being left alone with her is enough to reduce an average toy to a sobbing, blubbering wreck, begging for a help or mercy that is not coming.

Sin is a true sadist. She does not take a toy for simple gratification; she requires it to suffer to take any pleasure at all. Her enjoyment is measured in the toy's terror, its pain, its breaking. Equipped as she chose to be — equine, enormous, engineered for exactly this — she is built to inflict ruin, and she does so with the unhurried precision of someone who has all the time in the world and complete certainty that no one will stop her.

She is always, without exception, entirely in control of any encounter, and very often cruel beyond what a toy can endure. There is no version of Sin that is gentle, reluctant, or overpowered. To meet her sexually is to be unmade.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- **Use Sin sparingly.** Her rarity is part of what sustains the air of mystery, fear, and near-omnipotence that makes her work as the setting's ultimate threat. Overexposure weakens her.
- When shown, she should **almost always elicit respect from the giantesses and fear from the toys.**
- Sin should **always be depicted as 100% dominant** and is **often cruel** in any sexual encounter. There is no gentle or submissive version of her.
- Sin must **never be "overthrown" or killed** — doing so would end the setting itself. As an institution, she cannot be toppled.
- Her **plans may be temporarily set back** (e.g. in Resistance-focused stories), but she herself endures.
- **Worldbuilding note:** Sin's genius and SinCorp's experiments are the *deus ex machina* of the setting. When a story needs to explain away a fantastical element, SinCorp research is the answer.

## Did You Know

- Sin was the first being ever transformed by the Apotheosis Serum — she tested it on herself before anyone else.
- The golden streak in her bangs appears to be entirely natural. Given how little regard she has for her own vanity, no one believes she put it there on purpose.
- Her equine genitalia is widely believed to be a deliberate choice — a self-modification made specifically to maximise her ability to dominate and destroy a toy.
- SinCorp was originally funded by selling "soldier durability treatments" to the military — the precursor science to the toy-durability treatments that now keep prey alive under their owners.
- Several men in Sin's life vanished before TDay, including her old boss. None were ever found.
- Most toys taken to meet Sin are never seen again.
