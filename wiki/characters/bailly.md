## Overview

In a world of towering, predatory giantesses, Bailly is the girl next door. She is a laquine — a rare natural cross of rabbit and equine — standing well over a dozen feet of muscle with a sweet, almost ditzy disposition that fits her surroundings about as poorly as anything could. While most of her kind chase down stray toys or claw their way up the social pile, Bailly goes about her life unbothered and unconcerned with such self-important goals. She does not pine for the old world the way the Resistance does; this is the only world she has ever really known, and she simply wants to make a good life in it.

That places her in an unusual position — one paw in the world of massive, dominant women, and one paw in something much closer to simple, loving family life. Some find her naive, some find her childish, and others just wonder how the big bunny can be so laid back.

## Appearance

At a glance, Bailly is an enormous black bunny: thick, angora-soft black fur, long floppy ears, messy shoulder-length blue hair, and deep emerald-green eyes set in a face far gentler than her frame. Her true nature as a laquine shows in her build and her endowment — like other laquines she reads as mostly rabbit, but carries the size, power, and equine anatomy of her stranger half.

What sets her apart from the other transformed women is muscle. Even under that dense fur, Bailly is visibly, seriously built — broad shoulders, defined abs, powerful hips and thighs, the physique of someone who trains hard and often rather than one merely gifted size by the serum. She moves with the easy confidence of a body that has never once failed her. As a laquine she carries an equine endowment rather than the humanoid or canine forms common among her peers, a detail that, combined with her rabbit's breeding instincts, makes her a more potent presence than her soft face ever suggests.

## Personality

Bailly's gentleness is the thing the new world cannot account for. It is not timidity and not shame — though she does blush in a rather adorable way — but a genuine, settled calm that most giantesses simply never developed. Where the others had power thrust on them in an instant and a savage appetite with it, Bailly was given time and guidance to grow into hers, and it shows. She is a gentle giant: not cruel for cruelty's sake, looking on toys as adorable little things to be cuddled and kept rather than broken. It is only unfortunate that her size and strength are so often more than the small can handle.

She can come across as a sweet ditz, and isn't especially scholarly, but there is steel underneath. In her youth she showed a real capacity for violence — a fixture of combat gyms and training halls, a daunting sparring partner despite the disarming smile — and most unsettling of all is how fast she can flip. Push the bun far enough and the gentle giant gives way to something cold and vicious without warning. It takes a great deal to get her there. It is genuinely frightening when something does.

As a laquine, Bailly carries a rabbit's breeding drive wedded to a stallion's prowess, and she makes no secret of wanting a family. She adores the idea of a cute little partner of her own and a houseful of children — an aspiration that, in the post-TDay world, is its own quiet kind of strange.

## History

### Before TDay

Bailly was young when the world ended.  Her family was ordinary: her mother, a pony, and her father, a black angora rabbit. What made Bailly unusual was written in her from birth. As a laquine she was already a natural hermaphrodite, but her particular parentage — pony and angora rabbit — left her missing the genes that regulate and cap growth from either side. She was always going to grow enormous. She was always going to keep growing.

### After TDay

When the Apotheosis Serum swept the world, it reached Bailly along with everyone else, completing her transformation to join the ranks of the titanic giantesses of the new world.

Her mother had a less gentle change.  She was caught in the first wave like every other woman, swelling with size and lust, and nearly crushed her husband in her confusion — until he managed to snap her out of it by calling her a silly nickname Bailly had coined for her in her youth. That single thread of recognition was enough. Bailly's mother remained careful with him in a way almost no transformed woman managed, and Bailly grew up with the rarest thing a girl of the new world could have: an intact, loving family. It shaped everything about how she sees the world.

Watching what unchecked aggression had done to other girls, Bailly's mother sought out a martial-arts teacher for her daughter — discipline and focus to balance Bailly's capacity for violence. That training became a lifelong anchor. When Bailly later moved into the city, she joined a combat gym and made an impression on the older girls there, but what she wanted was something more peaceful. She found it teaching at a yoga studio, where her martial background helped her both instruct and handle the occasional entitled patron looking to throw her weight around. In time the studio was handed down to her, and Bailly — now the owner of her own small business, rebranded **Limber Buns Yoga** — finally had the time and means to focus on what she actually wants: a family of her own.

## As a Big Bun

Bailly is a gentle but intense domme. She has no need for cruelty or sadism — her size and presence are overwhelming entirely on their own. She loves to tease, using her body, her musk, and her sheer physical scale to push a toy past what it can handle, and she can be sweet and loving even while making it painfully clear how insignificant a toy is beside her. Degrading little nicknames come out as genuine affection; the moments she forgets her own strength and overwhelms a partner by accident become an excuse to fuss over the small thing afterward.

She finds eating a toy cruel and wasteful — she would much rather keep one and enjoy it than trade it for a moment's satisfaction. Feral livestock is another matter; her enormous body needs the protein, and she'll snack on a goat happily enough even while it kicks. What she truly craves is breeding: the feel of filling a tiny partner with her seed, the rabbit-deep satisfaction of the idea that she is impregnating something so small and precious. Any toy of her own is likely to be both modified to survive her and chipped to carry for her.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Bailly's signature is **breeding**, and the gentle-giant dynamic that surrounds it. Where most dominant women of the new world define themselves by cruelty, Bailly defines herself by appetite and affection — she wants to keep her partners, not break them, and the throughline of an encounter with her is overwhelming size paired with genuine, doting warmth. She teases relentlessly with her body, her scale, and her musk, savoring how completely a toy is enveloped by her, and she takes a rabbit's primal delight in the act of breeding above all else: the thought of impregnating something so much smaller than herself is the center of what she wants. The danger is never malice; it is simply that her gentleness operates at a scale a small body can barely survive, and that even her tenderness can be too much. A partner she keeps is one she modifies and chips, not to ruin but to make able to give her the family she so badly wants.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Bailly is a **gentle giant** — avoid portraying her as cruel and sadistic like other girls. She cuddles, teases, and leaves toys overwhelmed and breathless rather than dead.
- She is **not part of the Resistance** and isn't looking to rescue anyone. She simply wishes other girls would take better care of their cute little toys and stop being so wasteful.
- Bailly is **always growing**. Inconsistencies in her size or musculature can simply be read as depicting her at different ages — the older she is, the taller and more muscular. As a rule of thumb, lean into that rather than worrying about a fixed number.
- **Avoid vore.** Bailly hates the idea of intentionally eating a toy, finding it wasteful and a poor use of something so adorable. She snacks on feral livestock instead, even while it's still alive.
- Her sweetness can give way to **sudden violence**, but it should be striking and rare — something has to genuinely anger her. When it happens, write her as cold, ruthless, and devastating.
- That capacity does not preclude **feats of strength and discipline** — her martial-arts training is its own kind of shocking, separate from any cruelty.
- Bailly **dislikes and avoids** anything to do with scat, vomit, or flatulence. She'll engage in light watersports and musk play, but her scent is clean sweat and bunny musk, not stink — and she sweats profusely when she trains, thanks to her size and muscle.

## Did You Know

- She lived with an **intact family** — her father survived her mother's transformation because she recognized a childhood nickname through the haze, one of the rare TDay reunions that ended in tenderness rather than tragedy.
- Bailly's lack of a growth-regulating gene is similar to a real-world **liger** — like the hybrid big cat, she grows continuously and packs on muscle far faster than a normal person, and no one knows how large she might ultimately become, or how much of that runaway growth she could pass to her children.
- She runs her own yoga studio, **Limber Buns Yoga**, the former owner having handed it down to her.
- Bailly once **killed another giantess with a single punch to the chest.** She doesn't like to talk about it.
- In the few times she has met Sin, she insists on calling the other bunny **"Sinthia,"** which annoys her greatly.
- *(Out of character: Bailly began as a Monk-class character on an old Neverwinter Nights server, which is where her martial-arts background comes from.)*
