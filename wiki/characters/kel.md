## Overview

Kel is a red panda and a Transformed giantess standing 17'2" tall — above the standard mega-city baseline but well within the infrastructure range most cities are built to accommodate. She is independent in the truest sense: not affiliated with SinCorp, not part of the Resistance, not claiming a territory in the Wilds.

She is, in the parlance of the Sinverse world, her own thing.

## Appearance

Kel shares the red panda species markings with Jay — rust and cream, the characteristic tail, the round ears — but the scale changes everything. At 17'2" she occupies a room differently than the people around her. She is aware of this. She does not always acknowledge it.

## Personality

Kel is direct. She does not perform warmth she does not feel, and she does not perform coldness she does not feel either. What you see is what is there. This quality is disarming to those who expect the Transformed to operate on performance and status.

Her empathy did not suppress fully during transformation. She has never publicly addressed this.

## Lore / History

### Before TDay

Kel's life before TDay has not been documented publicly. She does not discuss it in detail.

### After TDay

Kel was transformed on TDay along with the rest of the world's female population. Her growth stopped at 17'2" — larger than most, smaller than many. She navigated the Chaos in her own way, and what emerged on the other side is the person who exists now.

The details of that period are her own.

## Did You Know

- Kel's height of 17'2" puts her in approximately the 70th percentile of Transformed heights. She is larger than most, but not conspicuously so within mega-city environments.
- She shares a species with Jay. Neither of them considers this a meaningful coincidence. It may or may not be one.
- Kel's empathy suppression was incomplete. The degree to which this shapes her behaviour is something those around her have differing theories about.
- She has never formally affiliated with any city, faction, or organisation. This is unusual enough that SinCorp has a file on her, though the file does not indicate why they consider her worth tracking.
- The Chronicles of Control series documents an extended interaction between Kel and a toy. It is, to date, the most detailed portrait of how Kel operates when she decides something is worth her sustained attention.
