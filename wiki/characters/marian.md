## Overview

Marian is an African wild dog giantess, a trusted secretary and functionary of [SinCorp](sincorp) who works close to [Sin](sin)'s own operations. To everyone who deals with her in that capacity she is the picture of reserve: composed, efficient, hair pinned up, the quiet competence that keeps an organization's machinery turning. It is a convincing exterior — and, as it turns out, only an exterior.

Beneath the secretary's poise sits an appetite as keen as any giantess's, kept lidded rather than absent. Given the right occasion, Marian lets the lid off, and the reserved functionary becomes something altogether wilder. She is a study in the gap between the composed surface and what it contains — a giantess who keeps her hair up at work and, off the clock, is very willing to let it down.

## Appearance

Marian is an African wild dog, her coat carrying the species' distinctive mottled patchwork of black, tan, and cream. She has bright green eyes and, by giantess standards, a modest stature — at around seven and a half feet she is one of the smaller of her kind, a Runt-scale giantess who nonetheless towers over any toy.

Her presentation is the most telling thing about her, because it changes. In her composed mode — which is to say, most of the time anyone sees her — she wears her hair up in a neat bun, the very image of the tidy, controlled professional. When her reserve slips, so does the bun: her hair comes down around her shoulders in a wild, beautiful mess, and the transformation in her bearing is as complete as the one in her hairstyle. The hair, up or down, is the outward sign of which Marian is present. Her transformation left her with humanoid anatomy.

## Personality

Marian is, on the surface, the model of reserve: quiet, composed, efficient, and professional. As a SinCorp secretary she carries herself with controlled competence, and that controlled quality is genuine — it isn't a mask in the sense of being false. It's a lid. The reserve is real, but it sits *on top* of something, holding it down rather than standing in for an absence.

What it holds down is appetite — the same hunger that runs through the giantesses of the new world, present in Marian all along and simply kept in check by temperament and circumstance. She is not a woman without those urges who discovered them; she is a woman who always had them and kept them tidily contained, the way she keeps her hair pinned and her desk in order. The containment is a habit, not a nature, and habits can be set aside.

When they are — when the right occasion gives her permission and reason to stop holding back — the other Marian comes out, and she is every bit a giantess of the new world: indulgent, hungry, and capable of a slow, sinister delight in a toy at her mercy. The striking thing is how completely the two coexist in her. She is not torn between them; she simply *oscillates*, composed and contained when the work calls for it, unleashed and indulgent when it doesn't, moving between the two with the ease of someone who was always both. The reserved secretary and the giantess with her hair down and a cowering toy in her fist are the same person, seen at different settings.

## History

### Before TDay

Little has been established about Marian's life before the world changed. Like all [the transformed](the-transformed), she was once ordinary, before the Apotheosis Serum remade her — though the composed, organized temperament that makes her so effective a functionary seems likely to have been hers all along.

### After TDay

Marian was transformed by the Apotheosis Serum on [TDay](tday) and, in the new world, found her place within [SinCorp](sin)'s organization — rising to become a trusted secretary and functionary working close to Sin's own operations. Efficient, discreet, and reliable, she made herself the kind of quiet competence an empire runs on, and earned a position near its centre.

It was at one of Sin's own house parties that the other side of Marian first showed itself. Moving through the gathering — past giantesses indulging themselves in every corner — she made her way to an upper room where Sin herself was presiding over a game: a tabletop role-playing session whose pieces, it turned out, were living shrunken toys, and whose hazards Sin made real, producing actual shrunken creatures to set upon a toy whenever a turn went against it. Drawn in and persuaded to play, Marian found the game working on her. With each turn her composure thinned, her reserve loosening notch by notch, until at last it gave way entirely — her hair coming down around her shoulders in a wild mess, a sinister grin spreading across her face, and a cowering toy trapped in her fist as she looked down at him. The lid, that night, came off for good.

She did not become a different person afterward so much as a more complete one. Back at her desk she is the composed secretary again, hair up, perfectly professional — but the appetite she uncovered that night is no longer something she pretends isn't there. She simply keeps it for her own time now, oscillating between the two faces as the occasion demands.

## As a SinCorp Functionary

Within SinCorp, Marian is the quiet competence near the centre — a trusted secretary and functionary who works close to Sin's operations and keeps the machinery running with discreet efficiency. Her value to the organization is exactly her reserve: she is reliable, organized, and unflappable, the sort of functionary who is trusted precisely because she does not call attention to herself.

That she is also, off the clock, every bit the indulgent giantess takes nothing away from her professional standing — if anything it makes her a more comfortable fit in Sin's world, where appetite is the native language and Marian merely speaks it fluently when the workday ends. She holds no grand title and commands no faction; her significance is in her proximity and her reliability, a trusted pair of hands close to the most important figure in the new world.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Marian's defining dynamic is **the contrast itself** — the reserved, composed professional who comes undone, the lid coming off an appetite that was always there. Much of what makes her compelling is that transition: the slow loosening of her control, the bun coming down, the shift from poised functionary to indulgent giantess with a sinister grin. The "letting her hair down," literal and figurative, is her signature register.

Once unleashed she enjoys the same dominance over toys the new world runs on, with a particular relish for a **slow, building** sort of cruelty — the savoured delight of composure giving way to appetite, a toy cowering in her fist while she looks down at him with that newly-uncovered hunger. Her smaller, Runt-scale size does nothing to diminish it; if anything the gap between her tidy exterior and what emerges from it is much of the point.
