## Overview

Juxta is one half of the inseparable pair behind one of the new world's toy shops — the steady, serious half. A white bunny with a sharp eye and a sharper focus, she is the toy trainer and the one who actually runs the storefront she shares with [Popo](popo), turning the fresh-caught toys Popo brings in into trained, sellable stock. Where Popo is all giggle and bounce, Juxta is composed and exacting, and the partnership works precisely because of the contrast: one to catch, one to make something of the catch.

The two are so close, and so constantly together, that nearly everyone calls them *sisters.* It isn't literal — they share no blood — but the word fits the bond better than "business partners" ever could. Juxta is the one who keeps the shop running, keeps the books straight, and keeps Popo herself pointed in the right direction when her partner's appetites get the better of her.

## Appearance

Juxta is a white bunny with short brown hair worn in a tufted, spiked-up style, her look finished with **blonde-dyed tips** that catch the light against the darker brown. She has steady brown eyes that miss very little. She stands nine and a half feet — the same height as Popo, a matched pair in scale as in everything else — though she carries it with a stillness and composure her bouncy partner entirely lacks.

The serum left her endowed in a humanoid form. On Juxta it reads as it does with everything about her: controlled, deliberate, an instrument she knows exactly how to use rather than an appetite that uses her.

## Personality

Juxta is the serious one, and in a partnership with Popo, someone has to be. She is composed, focused, and businesslike — the steady center of gravity around which her partner's chaos orbits. That seriousness is not coldness so much as discipline: she has a shop to run, stock to prepare, and a perpetually distractible partner to manage, and she meets all three with the same unflustered competence.

Her sternness serves three purposes at once, and she wields it deliberately. It keeps **Popo** in check, hauling her partner back to task when her appetite has scattered her wits. It keeps the **business** running, because someone has to mind the money, the inventory, and the doors. And it makes her an effective **trainer**, because breaking a caught toy into his new life calls for exactly the firm, unwavering authority she comes by naturally. Juxta is not playing a role when she's stern; it is simply who she is, and she has found three good uses for it.

For all that, she is not the one the shop's toys fear most — and she knows it. Juxta is firm, demanding, and exacting, but she is also predictable, and a toy can learn what she wants and how to give it. It is sunny, giggling Popo, with her bottomless appetite and her obliviousness to her own brutality, who teaches the toys what real dread is. Juxta's discipline is a fixed thing you can navigate. Popo's affection is a flood you cannot.

## History

### Before TDay

Little is recorded of Juxta's life before the change. What carried across was evidently the temperament — the focus, the head for order, the steadiness — the qualities that would later make her the anchor of a two-woman business and the manager of a partner who needs managing.

### After TDay

In the new world's economy, a caught toy is not yet a sellable one, and Juxta built her half of the partnership on that gap. She and Popo set up their shop as a single operation spanning the whole pipeline — Popo to range out and catch, Juxta to train the catch and run the sales — and it has thrived on the completeness of the arrangement and the closeness of the two women behind it. Juxta is the one who made it a *business* rather than just a pair of bunnies with a building, and the one who keeps it one. The partnership has lasted because it is also a friendship so deep the world assumes they must be family.

## As a Toy Trainer and Shopkeeper

Juxta's work is two jobs that feed each other. As the shop's **trainer**, she takes the frightened, freshly-caught toys Popo hauls in and breaks them into their new existence — teaching them what is expected, what is forbidden, and what they now are, with the firm and unwavering authority the task demands. A toy that leaves her training is a toy fit to be sold, which is the entire point of the enterprise.

As the **shopkeeper**, she runs everything else: the storefront, the stock, the transactions, the books. The catch→train→sell pipeline the two of them operate under one roof runs on her organisation, and she is the public face customers deal with when they come to buy. Popo brings the raw material in the back; Juxta sends the finished product out the front. It is a clean division of labour between two women who could not be less alike and could not be more inseparable.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Juxta's register is **control and discipline** — the firm, deliberate dominance of a trainer rather than the runaway appetite of her partner. Her satisfaction is in mastery: in taking something unruly and frightened and shaping it into exactly what she wants, methodically and on her own terms. She is demanding and exacting, and an encounter with her is defined by structure, expectation, and consequence rather than by overwhelming hunger. It is, pointedly, the opposite of Popo's chaos — and the deliberate contrast between the two is the whole shape of the pair.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Juxta is the **serious, composed** half of the pair — disciplined and businesslike, the steady center to Popo's chaos.
- Her sternness has **three uses**: managing Popo, running the business, and training toys. Write it as deliberate competence, not coldness.
- Despite being the stern one, she is **not** the one toys fear most — that's Popo. Juxta is firm but predictable; a toy can learn to navigate her. Lean on that distinction.
- Her register is **control and discipline** — a methodical trainer-dominant, the deliberate opposite of Popo's runaway lust.
- She is not literally Popo's sister — inseparable partners called sisters out of affection.
- Her look: white bunny, short brown hair in a **tufted/spiked** style, **blonde-dyed tips**, steady brown eyes.

## Did You Know

- Juxta and [Popo](popo) were the very first two characters ever created in the setting.
- Juxta, Popo, and [Sin](sin) were all named after the theme of **juxtaposition** — a core principle of the setting. Juxta's name carries it most directly of all.
- Juxta and Popo are called sisters, but it's affection, not blood — they're inseparable partners, not relatives.
- Juxta is the stern one, yet she is *not* the one the shop's toys fear most. Her discipline is predictable; it's Popo's oblivious, bottomless affection that teaches real dread.
- She runs the business end of the shop entirely — training, storefront, stock, and books — while Popo handles the catching.
- Part of her job is simply keeping Popo focused, hauling her partner back on task whenever her appetite scatters her wits.
