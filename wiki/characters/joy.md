## Overview

Joy is a clone of [Jay](jay), grown in a SinCorp laboratory at the personal direction of [Sin](sin) and built to be something Jay never was: a breeder. She was [Sin's](sin) answer to a specific problem — the rarity, and therefore the value, of red panda [toys](toys) — and she was engineered to solve it, a female red panda capable of siring offspring even on partners who carry no breeding modification of their own. By the cold logic of the project she should have been a triumph.

She was not. Joy can breed, prolifically and without assistance — but she does not produce red pandas, the one thing she was made to do. To Sin, that made her a failed experiment, and Sin discarded the judgment as casually as she'd commissioned the work. To Joy, who was built knowing exactly what she was for, that verdict broke something. Recently, and unsteadily, she has come out the other side of that break pointed in a direction Sin did not intend: against her.

## Appearance

Joy looks, unmistakably, like a giantess-scaled echo of Jay — the same red panda colouring and markings, the same features, rendered at well over eleven feet and built on a breeder's generous frame. Anyone who knows the original would place her as his at a glance, which is precisely what she is.

The one detail that distinguishes her is her crest: where Jay's is ordinary, Joy's carries a pronounced heart-shaped centre, a small flourish in the design that has become the easiest way to tell the clone from the source. She is endowed as the breeder she was built to be, humanoid in form, and carries herself at a scale that makes her engineered purpose plain. Notably, she is not a [transformed](the-transformed) woman at all — she was *grown* to this size in a vat, never touched by the serum, a deliberately built large female rather than one the [Apotheosis Serum](apotheosis-serum) made.

## Personality

Joy is brittle and defiant, and she wears the chip on her shoulder where everyone can see it. She came into existence with a purpose stamped on her and a maker who decided she'd failed at it, and that is the wound the whole of her personality has formed around. She is quick to bristle, quick to take a slight, and quicker still to assume one is coming — a woman braced, at all times, for being found wanting again.

Underneath the defiance is the thing that drives her: a need to prove the verdict wrong. Sin called her a failure, and Joy cannot let that stand. It is not conscience that moves her and it is not peace — it is spite, sharp and personal, the determination of someone who was thrown away to make her discarder regret it. That engine is what nearly destroyed her, and it is what now, redirected, makes her dangerous to the woman who built her. She is loyal, in her prickly way, to the few who treated her as something other than a result on a chart. She is merciless toward the one who didn't.

## History

### Before TDay

Joy has no "before." She was grown long after [TDay](tday), in a SinCorp facility, from material taken from Jay — a clone commissioned to order rather than a person with a past. Whatever history she has is entirely the history of the project that made her, and of what happened when that project was declared a failure.

### After TDay

Sin built Joy to address a problem the new world had created. Red panda toys had proven rare and, in their rarity, valuable; Sin's solution was to engineer a dedicated breeder — a female red panda, vat-grown to scale, capable of siring offspring even on partners without a breeding modification, an efficient engine for producing more of a prized line. On the narrow question of whether Joy could breed, the experiment succeeded completely.

On the question that mattered to Sin, it failed. Joy's offspring did not come out red pandas. The one trait the entire project existed to propagate was the one thing Joy could not reliably pass on, and to Sin that rendered the whole effort a dead end. She labelled Joy a failed experiment and moved on with the indifference she shows any result that doesn't serve.

Joy did not move on. Being made for a purpose and then told she had failed at it — by her own creator, the closest thing to a god she had — did something to her that the cold framing of "failed experiment" does no justice. She came apart. She fixated on the idea that the fault was not hers but the *source material's* — Jay's "bad" DNA — and convinced herself that if she could only breed the original successfully, she could prove to Sin that she was not a failure after all. The fixation on Jay grew obsessive and frightening, and how far it went before it was interrupted is something Joy does not discuss and no one else will confirm.

What interrupted it was [Libby](libby). Whatever happened or didn't, it was Libby who reached Joy in the spiral — who talked her down from the obsession, and then did something Sin would have found far more inconvenient than a failed clone: she turned Joy's spite away from Jay and toward the woman who had thrown her away. Joy did not become gentle. She did not become a believer. But she came out of it aimed at Sin instead of at her own brother, and that was enough.

## As a Breeder

Joy's engineered function is real, whatever Sin made of the results. She is a true breeder, able to sire prolifically and, unusually, to do so on partners who carry no breeding modification at all — a capability built into her from the vat, and one that makes her a remarkable thing regardless of the colour of the offspring. In a world where [reproduction](reproduction) is a managed, technological process and the toy population is in decline, a body that can breed unassisted is no small thing; it is only Sin's specific demand for red pandas that ever cast it as a failure.

Joy's own feelings about the function are tangled. It is the thing she was made for and the thing she "failed" at, and she cannot entirely separate her sense of her own worth from it — which is exactly the hook the Resistance, and Libby in particular, had to work against to reach her.

## As a Resistance Asset

Joy's alignment with the [Resistance](the-resistance) is real but begrudging, and it does not look like anyone else's. She is not [Tempered](tempered) — she has no surviving well of empathy driving her to save toys, no conscience pulling her toward the work. What she has is a score to settle. She helps the Resistance because the Resistance is aimed at Sin, and hurting Sin is the only thing that quiets the verdict ringing in her head. It is spite doing the work that conscience does in others, and it makes her a volatile asset: motivated, capable, and prone to making it personal in a movement that survives by staying impersonal.

She is also, recently turned and still raw, not entirely stable. The break Sin caused has not fully healed, and the same engine that points her at Sin could turn on anything if it slips. The Resistance — and Libby most of all — handle her accordingly: valued for what she can do and what she knows of SinCorp's breeding programmes, watched for the cracks that have not yet closed.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Joy's defining theme is **breeding** — it is what she was built for, what her body is engineered around, and the thing most tangled up with her fragile sense of worth. Her unusual ability to sire without a partner's modification makes her a singular presence in that register, and the act carries a weight for her it would not for anyone else: every encounter brushes up against the purpose she was made to fulfil and told she'd failed. That can make her intense, proprietary, and difficult to separate from the need to prove something — a dynamic shadowed, always, by the brittle defiance she carries everywhere else.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Joy is **not a transformed woman.** She was vat-grown to her size as a clone, never touched by the serum. Don't write her as an apotheosis giantess; her scale and endowment are engineered, not transformed.
- Her core wound is being declared a **failed experiment** by Sin. Almost everything about her — the defiance, the spite, the breeding fixation — traces back to it. She should never read as simply content.
- Her Resistance alignment is **spite, not conscience.** She is not Tempered and not kind-hearted; she works against Sin to prove Sin wrong, and that distinction matters.
- She is **recently turned and still unstable.** Lean into the rawness — the obsession redirected rather than resolved, the cracks not fully closed.
- The **Jay fixation** is canon, but whether it was ever acted on is deliberately left unstated. Keep it ambiguous unless you have reason not to.
- Her relationship to **Libby** is the load-bearing one — Libby is the person who pulled her back and the closest thing she has to trust.

## Did You Know

- Joy is a clone of Jay, grown from his own material in a SinCorp lab — which makes the original, in effect, her brother.
- She can breed without a partner needing any breeding modification at all, a capability engineered into her from the vat and rare even in a world built around managed reproduction.
- Sin considers Joy a failed experiment for one reason only: her offspring don't come out red pandas, the single trait the entire project existed to propagate.
- The easiest way to tell Joy from Jay is her crest — hers has a distinct heart-shaped centre.
- Joy sided with the Resistance out of spite against Sin, not empathy. She is not Tempered, which makes her motivation unlike almost anyone else in the movement.
- It was Libby who talked Joy down from her fixation on Jay and turned her spite toward Sin instead — arguably a far greater inconvenience to Sin than the failed clone ever was.
- Despite her giantess scale, Joy is classed among the untransformed: she was built large, not changed into it.
