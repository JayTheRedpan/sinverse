## Overview

Zuzu is one of the Transformed who did not come through the change intact. A wolf whose mind was all but destroyed by the volatile TDay Variant of the serum, she emerged on the far side of her transformation as a towering, animalistic thing that moves on instinct alone. Among the women of SinCorp she is spoken of less as a person than as one of the **ferals** — dangerous, unpredictable Transformed who have lost whatever self they once had, and whom even Sin's enforcers give a wide berth. Her ground lies in the off-limits wild zones beyond the city walls — territory marked as forbidden precisely because of what roams it.

What makes Zuzu particularly unsettling is that she did not need to be enormous to end up this way. At a shade over twenty-three feet she is no larger than countless functioning giantesses who live ordinary lives in the mega-cities — yet her mind is gone all the same, a reminder that the instability of the TDay Variant was never strictly a matter of size.

## Appearance

Zuzu is a wolf with muddy brown fur over most of her body, fading to a lighter underbelly. Her hands end in claws that are strikingly large even for a Transformed of her scale. She has heterochromatic eyes — one a reddish brown, the other green — set in a face that rarely carries any human expression. A short crop of deep red hair sits atop her head, vivid and incongruous against the dull brown of her coat. She is most often seen hunched forward onto all fours, carrying herself like a beast rather than a woman, with the low, prowling gait of something that has forgotten how to stand as a person.

Like the other transformed women, the serum left her endowed in place of what it took — a canine shape, in her case — though on a creature this far gone it reads less as anatomy than as one more part of the predator.

## Personality

There is, in any meaningful sense, very little personality left to describe. The transformation that remade Zuzu also hollowed her out: higher thought, language, and self-awareness are essentially gone, leaving raw instinct in their place. She does not scheme, speak, or play the cruel social games the other Transformed delight in — she reacts, to movement and scent and the presence of prey, and little else. What remains is closer to a wild animal than a woman: territorial, reactive, and without the human cognition that might let her be reasoned with or predicted.

There is no malice in her, in the way the word usually means — malice requires a self to do the wanting, and Zuzu's is gone. What is left is appetite without the person who used to own it, which is in its way far worse: she cannot be bargained with, appealed to, or talked down, because there is no longer anyone home to hear it.

## History

### Before TDay

Nothing of Zuzu's life before the change has ever been established. Whatever she was — whoever she was — did not survive her transformation in any form able to tell it, and the feral she became carries no memory of it that anyone has been able to reach. Like a handful of the most damaged Transformed, she is known to the world only by what walked out of TDay, never by what walked in.

### After TDay

Zuzu was caught in the first wave of TDay and transformed by the unstable variant released that day — the formulation responsible for the era's wild inconsistencies, and for the cognitive damage that so often accompanied them. In most who suffered that damage it came paired with macro scale; in Zuzu it came without it. She grew only to giantess size, but her mind collapsed almost completely all the same, and she emerged one of the feral Transformed rather than a functioning part of Sin's new order.

She has lived apart ever since, roaming the off-limits wild zones where the ferals are left to themselves. SinCorp personnel treat any encounter with her — or any of her kind — as a serious hazard, and the ground she ranges is marked forbidden. She is referenced in SinCorp's own incident records as having left at least one captive mind-destroyed, and the ferals as a group are considered too dangerous to engage at all.

## As a Predator

Zuzu is a predator in the most literal, instinct-driven sense the new world produces. She does not hunt for sport or cruelty the way the lucid Transformed do — she hunts because that is all that is left of her, and a toy who strays into her territory is simply prey that has wandered close. There are no games, no taunts, no bargains dressed up as mercy; there is only the cold arithmetic of something much larger that has caught something much smaller.

What befalls those she catches is the stuff of the worst stories the Wild Toys tell each other. A captive does not come back from Zuzu intact — at best mind-broken, more often not at all — and the records that mention her note victims left hollowed in the same way she herself was hollowed, as though the damage in her propagates outward into whatever she gets her claws around. She is best understood not as a character with intent but as a hazard of the wild zones: a thing that other survivors fear, avoid, and pray never to blunder into.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Unlike the other women of the new world, Zuzu has no preferences to speak of — no chosen kinks, no signature dynamic, no tastes she pursues. Her ruined mind leaves her with instinct in place of intent, and an encounter with her is shaped entirely by that: overwhelming size, animal drive, and the absolute absence of anyone to appeal to. Whatever a given story makes of her belongs to its creator; there is no deliberate dynamic written into Zuzu herself, only the blunt, mindless fact of a predator and her prey.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- **She is non-verbal.** Zuzu has no dialogue. She doesn't banter, taunt, or negotiate the way other Transformed do — she acts, she doesn't talk.
- **She is feral, not clever-cruel.** Don't write her plotting or savoring cruelty as a personality trait. She runs on instinct, closer to a force of nature or an apex animal than a character with intent.
- **She is an environmental threat.** She works best as a hazard of the wild zones — something other characters fear, avoid, or blunder into — rather than someone with her own social arc.
- **Her damage is not about size.** Zuzu is giantess-scale, not macro, and that's the point — she's proof the TDay Variant could ruin a mind without a hundred feet of body to blame it on. Don't write her as a macro giant.

## Did You Know

- Zuzu is one of the **ferals** — Transformed whose minds the serum destroyed rather than merely hardened. SinCorp guards refer to them only by that name and consider the zones they inhabit too dangerous to patrol.
- She is **not macro.** At just over twenty-three feet she is no larger than many functioning giantesses, yet her mind collapsed all the same — a stark example that the TDay Variant's instability was never strictly tied to size.
- Sin reportedly marks any ground near the ferals as **off-limits** — one of the few concessions to danger in a world she otherwise rules unchallenged.
- Her mismatched eyes — one reddish brown, one green — and the incongruous crop of red hair are the only vivid colour on an otherwise dull brown coat, and the only things about her that still read as having once been someone.
