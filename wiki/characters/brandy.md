## Overview

Brandy is the new world's most aspirational influencer — at least according to Brandy, and to the legions of younger giantesses who hang on her every post. A golden retriever with a megawatt smile and a phone permanently in hand, she has built a personal brand out of living fabulously in the post-TDay world: showing off her toys like the season's must-have accessory, dolling them up for her followers, and dispensing trends, opinions, and toy "reviews" to an audience that treats her word as gospel. She is bubbly, bratty, vain, and relentlessly *on*, and beneath the sunny influencer gloss she is exactly as shallow and as casually cruel as the role rewards.

She is not a power player. Her reviews don't move markets, and the serious women of the new world find her ridiculous. But to the young giantesses coming up — the ones who want the fabulous life and take their cues on how to live it from someone who seems to have it figured out — Brandy is the trend, and the trend is Brandy.

## Appearance

Brandy is a golden retriever standing fifteen feet tall, and she is groomed to within an inch of her life: a lustrous golden coat, an influencer's flawless styling, and an outfit that is always, pointedly, *current.* She looks exactly like what she is — a woman whose entire public existence is a photo waiting to be taken — and she maintains the look with the discipline of someone who knows the camera is always rolling.

Her signature is a **choker collar** bearing her brand icon: the combined male-and-female symbol that has come to represent the towering women of the new world, shown piercing through a much smaller, separate male symbol. It is at once her logo, her statement of belief, and her favourite accessory, and she is rarely seen without it at her throat. The serum left her endowed in a canine form to match her breed — a detail she is neither shy about nor crude with, treating it as simply one more fabulous attribute among many.

## Personality

Brandy is the stereotype made flesh: the fake, bratty, popular-girl archetype the old world would have recognised instantly, scaled up to fifteen feet and handed dominion over a population of toys. She is vain, shallow, and performatively sweet — warm and gushing for the camera, dismissive and bored the instant it stops. She lives through her phone, narrating her own life as content, and is given to answering even in person in clipped phrases and a steady stream of emojis, as though everything were a comment thread.

Underneath the bubbly brand is the casual cruelty the influencer role rewards and conceals. Brandy does not hate her toys, exactly; she simply does not think of them as anything more than props in the production of her own image — accessories to be styled, displayed, rated, and replaced as the content demands. The sunny delivery is real, and so is the thoughtlessness behind it. She is not a sadist in the manner of the truly cruel Transformed; she is something more banal and, in its way, more dispiriting — a woman for whom a toy's entire worth is measured in engagement.

## History

### Before TDay

Little is documented of Brandy's life before the change, and she rarely references it — the past is not on-brand. What is evident is that she carried the influencer instinct across the transformation intact: the need to perform, to be watched, to convert her life into something an audience consumes. TDay simply handed her a far bigger stage and a far more captivating subject.

### After TDay

The new world was, for someone like Brandy, an opportunity. The transformation gave her the towering, fabulous body the new order prizes, and it gave her toys — and toys, she quickly grasped, were content. She built her channel on the fantasy of the perfectly fabulous post-TDay life: the enviable home, the enviable look, the enviable stable of toys, all of it packaged for an audience of younger giantesses hungry for exactly that aspiration. She rode the new world's appetites straight into a personal brand, and has been performing it ever since.

## As an Influencer

Brandy's content runs on toys, and on three formats above all.

She **shows them off** — parading her toys for her followers the way the old world's influencers flaunted designer bags, each one a status piece in an outfit, an accessory whose value is how covetable it makes her look. On camera, a handful of favourites recur often enough that her audience knows them by name, little cast members in the ongoing production of Brandy's fabulous life.

She **dolls them up** — makeup tutorials and styling segments performed *on* her toys, dressing and prettying them for the feeds, turning them into living mannequins for whatever look she is selling that week. It is demeaning by design and adoring in tone, the two never quite separable, which is exactly the register her followers tune in for.

And she **reviews them** — rating toys for her audience like products, holding forth on their qualities and failings with the breezy authority of someone whose opinion her viewers take as fact. A good review is a moment in the spotlight. The reviews shape what her young, aspiring followers want in a toy of their own, and in that narrow, trend-setting way Brandy genuinely moves tastes, if not markets.

What the highlight reel does not show is the churn. The favourites are curated; behind them is a steady turnover of toys acquired and discarded as the content requires — the ones who didn't film well, didn't last, or simply stopped being useful to the brand, edited out of the fabulous life as cleanly as a bad take. To her followers, Brandy's stable is a glamorous, stable cast. The casualties never make the post.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Brandy's register is **domination as content** — humiliation, display, and the reduction of a toy to an accessory or a prop. Her cruelty is aesthetic rather than physical: she delights in dressing a toy up, showing him off, rating and ranking him, and making it unmistakable that his entire purpose is to make *her* look good. The dynamic is built on objectification in the most literal influencer sense — the toy as a status piece, a mannequin, a product on review — performed with a bubbly, demeaning sweetness that never acknowledges the person underneath. The camera is almost always implied; for Brandy, a thing barely happened if it wasn't content.

## Usage Guide

*Notes for content creators working with this character.*

- Brandy is the **fake-popular-girl stereotype** at giantess scale: vain, bratty, performatively sweet, phone-obsessed, emoji-speaking. Lean into the influencer satire.
- Her cruelty is **casual and aesthetic, not sadistic.** She treats toys as props and accessories — styled, displayed, reviewed, replaced — rather than as victims to torment. The horror is in the thoughtlessness.
- Her **brand icon** — the new world's combined gender symbol piercing a smaller male symbol — is her logo and her look, worn on a **choker collar** as her signature. Use it.
- The **favourites-on-camera, churn-behind-it** split is key. Her followers see a glamorous recurring cast; the discarded toys never make the highlight reel.
- She is **aspirational to younger giantesses**, not respected by the powerful. She sets trends for the up-and-coming, not policy for the order.
- Keep the platform **generic** — her "channel," her "followers," "the feeds" — rather than naming a specific service.
