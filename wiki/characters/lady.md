## Overview

Lady is a border collie giantess and one of the [Huntresses](the-paws) — the Paws — who range into the [Wilds](the-wilds) to capture toys for the trade. She is, in the most literal sense, a creature built for the work: a herding dog to her bones, she lives to run and lives to chase, and the profession that terrifies its quarry is, to Lady, the best game there is.

What sets her apart among the Paws is not how she catches toys but how much she *enjoys* the catching. For Lady the hunt is never just a pursuit ending in a bag; it's a game of running, toying, and herding her quarry — drawing the chase out, steering her prey where she wants him, savouring every moment of a nightmare that is, from her side of it, pure delight. Warm and well-liked among her sisters, heedless and relentless toward the toys she runs down, Lady is a huntress who has found in the new world's most dangerous trade exactly the life her instincts always wanted.

## Appearance

Lady is a border collie, standing around thirteen feet — a lean, athletic giantess built for distance and speed rather than mass. She has the alert, ready frame of a working dog, medium-length brown hair, and warm brown eyes that are usually bright with the eagerness of someone halfway into her next run.

Her most characteristic feature is one of motion: when she's excited or working hard — which is much of the time — she pants, her long flat tongue lolling from her mouth, sometimes dripping as she goes. It's the unmistakable look of a dog in her element, and on Lady it reads as exactly what it is: a giantess thoroughly, physically delighted to be doing what she does. Her transformation left her with canine anatomy.

## Personality

Lady is warm, eager, and friendly — genuinely well-liked among the Paws, the kind of huntress others are glad to have at their back. There's an openness to her, an easy good nature that makes her good company on the trail and a trusted sister in a profession where trust is the only real currency. She honours the Paws' unwritten code without having to think about it, and the others know it.

But the warmth sits alongside something single-minded and, for her quarry, merciless: Lady *lives* for the chase. The herding-dog instinct runs all the way through her, and a toy on the run lights her up in a way nothing else does. Crucially, she doesn't just catch — she *plays*. Where another huntress might run a toy down and bag him as efficiently as possible, Lady draws it out: chasing, herding, steering her prey this way and that, toying with him, letting the pursuit run long because the pursuit is the best part. What is, to the toy, an exhausting and terrifying nightmare is, to Lady, a delightful game with a very satisfying ending — and she sees no contradiction in that at all, because to her a toy is quarry, and quarry is for chasing.

That same cheerful single-mindedness makes her thoroughly heedless of a toy's comfort, hunt or no hunt. She is not cruel in a cold or calculated way; she simply doesn't *consider* the small creatures in her orbit as things whose comfort matters, treating them with the unthinking carelessness of a big dog who doesn't notice what she's sitting on. A toy can find himself stuck along for the ride of Lady's day — even, on one memorable occasion, still attached to her from the night before and dragged along for the whole of her morning run — without Lady sparing a thought for how he's faring. The good nature and the heedlessness are the same trait wearing two faces: Lady is having a wonderful time, and it would not occur to her that anyone in her grip might not be.

## History

### Before TDay

Little has been established about Lady's life before the world changed. Like all the transformed, she was once ordinary, before the Apotheosis Serum remade her — though the restless, run-loving energy that defines her seems a natural fit for the border collie she became.

### After TDay

Lady was transformed by the Apotheosis Serum on TDay, and in the new world she found her way to a profession that fit her instincts like nothing else could: the hunt. She took up the dangerous, lucrative trade of the [Paws](the-paws), ranging into the Wilds after the toys that command the highest prices at market — and discovered that the work she'd be best at was also the work she loved most. For a giantess who lives to run and chase, being paid to do exactly that, in the most demanding country the new world offers, is less a job than a calling.

She has made herself a well-regarded huntress and a welcome sister among the Paws, equally at home on a solitary long-distance ranging or banded into a temporary pack for a bigger haul. Like all her trade she equips herself from [SinCorp](sincorp)'s capture catalogue, but the gear is the least of what makes her good: Lady's real tools are her legs, her nose, her stamina, and the bottomless joy she takes in the chase.

## As a Huntress

Among the Paws, Lady is known for the long pursuit — running her quarry down over distance, wearing him out, herding him toward the ground she's chosen rather than simply pouncing. Her border collie heritage shows in everything about her method: the tirelessness, the steering and circling, the patient management of a fleeing toy as if working a flock of one. She is a ranger who covers enormous ground, and a toy who thinks he's escaped Lady has usually only given her a longer, more enjoyable game.

She fits the sisterhood's loose structure comfortably — solo by preference for the everyday ranging, where she can run as far and as freely as she likes, but a reliable and well-liked partner when a haul calls for a banded pack. Her warmth makes her easy to work with and her stamina makes her worth working with; among a fellowship held together entirely by reputation, Lady's is good. The toys she brings back arrive thoroughly run-down and worse for the experience, having served, whether they knew it or not, as the playthings in a game Lady enjoyed enormously.

## Preferences

*The kinks and dynamics that define this character's encounters.*

Lady's defining dynamic is **the chase as a game** — the herding-dog delight of running, toying with, and steering a fleeing toy, drawing out a pursuit that is terror to her quarry and pure joy to her. The savoured, drawn-out hunt is her whole register: not a quick catch but a long one, full of circling and herding and the satisfaction of a prey she's playing with as much as pursuing, ending the way she likes.

Threaded through it is a cheerful **heedlessness of toys' comfort** — she treats the small with the unthinking carelessness of a big working dog, dragging them through her day without a thought for how they fare. Her canine traits run all the way through: the panting, the lolling tongue, the boundless physical energy, and the simple animal joy of running something down. The toy stuck to her for the length of a morning run is the perfect picture of her — she's having a wonderful time, and his comfort never crosses her mind.
