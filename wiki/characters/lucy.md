## Overview

Lucy is a ram and one of the new world's self-made monsters. Where most of [the transformed](the-transformed) were simply *changed* on [TDay](tday) — swept up by the serum into giant, hungry, half-[feral](ferals) predators — Lucy was an anomaly twice over. The transformation barely touched her: she came out small, modestly endowed, and, far stranger, with her mind fully intact while the women around her lost themselves to instinct. That cold clarity in the middle of the apocalypse was the most dangerous thing about her. While everyone else became an animal, Lucy stayed a person — a calculating, ambitious one — and she used that advantage to do something almost no one else could: she made herself into a giantess on purpose.

Today she is over sixty feet of deliberate, power-hungry appetite, and she revels in every inch of the size she schemed and clawed her way into.

## Appearance

Lucy is a ram whose coloring runs cream across most of her fur, deepening to a dark brown as it reaches her face, her hands, and her cloven hooves. Two pronounced horns curl around the sides of her head, her ears poking out from within their enclosure, and a small tuft of orange hair sweeps across the top. Her eyes are brown and slit-pupiled, the unmistakable goatish stare — once shy and skittish, now flat and assessing.

Pre-transformation she was, by the standards of the new world, almost comically ordinary: a head shorter than her own professor, a "stunted" endowment that disappointed her where it should have been a relief. After she forced the change to take hold properly, all of that vanished into scale — sixty-plus feet of ram, with the oversized humanoid endowment to match the size she'd demanded.

## Personality

Lucy's defining trait is the one the serum left her: a mind that never switched off. In the chaos of TDay she was clear-headed where every other woman was drowning in instinct, and she recognized immediately what that clarity was worth. She is calculating, patient, and coldly opportunistic — a sharp student's intelligence wedded to a predator's complete absence of mercy. She thinks in terms of advantage, leverage, and position, and she is willing to use people without hesitation and discard them without a second thought once they've served their purpose.

What drives her is ambition, plain and hungry. From the first hours of the new world Lucy wanted to be the biggest, the strongest, the apex — "queen of the jungle," in her own framing — and she pursued that goal with a single-mindedness that the genuinely feral around her could never have managed. Now that she has the size she schemed for, she revels in it openly: in her power, her scale, and the helplessness of anything small enough to fit in her hand. The skittish, deferential student is long gone. What replaced her is someone who looked at the end of the world and saw an opportunity to rule a piece of it.

## History

### Before TDay

Lucy was a college sophomore — a biology student, shy and clever, the kind of pupil who hung on her professor's every word and clopped her hooves nervously when she spoke. She was her biology professor's prize student, and she'd learned more from him than coursework: the instincts of a researcher, the habit of gathering data, the cold logic of the lab. None of that seemed like it would ever matter beyond a grade.

### TDay

When the serum swept across the world and the women around her erupted into giant, feral predators, Lucy changed too — but barely. Where her classmates became towering monsters lost to instinct, Lucy emerged only a little altered: still small, modestly endowed, and, uniquely, still herself. Her thoughts, she would say, were *clearer* than ever.

She did not treat this as the stroke of luck others might have. To Lucy, being small in a world of giants was a death sentence deferred, and being sane was simply a tool. Acting on the researcher's instincts her professor had drilled into her, she gathered samples — blood and fluids harvested from the transformed, some of it taken by force — and went looking for the one person on campus who might understand them: her old biology professor, hiding in a basement lab.

She coerced him into helping her, half threat and half appeal, and together they isolated the mutagen behind the transformation before it could decay. Then she had him re-trigger and amplify the change in her, gambling her life on a crude distillation to make herself bigger than any rival she'd seen. The gamble paid off. Lucy erupted into a true giantess — and the professor who had made it possible became the first to learn exactly what she intended to do with her new size. He did not survive the experience.

### After TDay

Having forced herself to the top of the food chain, Lucy embraced the new world rather than resisting it. The talk of "restoring order" that she'd used to win her professor's cooperation fell away the moment she had what she wanted; what remained was raw ambition and appetite. She roams the new world as one of its larger predators, reveling in her hard-won scale and answering to no faction and no cause but her own advancement.
