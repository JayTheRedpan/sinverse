## Overview

A Matriarch is the ruler of a [mega-city](mega-cities) — a single giantess who holds total authority over one of the new world's island-nations. Where the old world divided power across parliaments, courts, and constitutions, the post-TDay mega-cities concentrate it in one figure. A Matriarch's word is the law of her city, her culture is its culture, and her appetites and convictions shape the lives of everyone within her walls, Transformed and toy alike.

There is no Matriarch of the world. Each rules only her own city, and the cities answer to no authority above them — save one. Above every Matriarch stands [Sin](sin), the **First Matriarch**, whose rare word overrides any of theirs.

## What Makes a Matriarch

A Matriarch is not simply the largest giantess in a city, nor the richest. Size and wealth help, but they are not the qualification. A Matriarch rules by two powers: her **power to command** — the force of will and presence to make others follow her and keep them following — and her **power to enforce the new world order**, in whatever manner she judges best.

This is a deliberately broad standard, and it produces wildly different rulers. One Matriarch may hold her city through raw terror, another through cunning and patronage, another through sheer magnetism or the loyalty of a devoted inner circle. What unites them is not method but result: each has proven she can command a city and keep it firmly within the new order. A giantess who can do those two things can become a Matriarch by almost any path. One who cannot will not hold the seat, however large she grows.

Because the qualification is command rather than any fixed rule of succession, the character of a city follows directly from the character of whoever can seize and hold it. The endless variety between mega-cities is, in large part, the variety between the women who rule them.

## Total Autonomy

Within her own city, a Matriarch's autonomy is effectively total. She sets its laws, defines its culture, directs its economy, and determines the status and treatment of the toys within it. The mega-cities operate far more like independent nations than members of any collective, and a Matriarch is their sovereign in the fullest sense — accountable, in the ordinary run of things, to no one.

That autonomy extends even to the one institution present in every city. Each city's [SinCorp](sincorp) branch operates with its own broad latitude — devising its own experiments, pursuing its own research, adding its own contributions to the cruelty and spectacle of the new world — and a Matriarch may direct her local branch toward whatever ends she desires, setting its scientists to work on the things she most wants made real. For all practical purposes, the laboratory in her city is hers to command.

The limit on that power is singular, and it is absolute. Should an order ever come down from Sin herself — a genuine rarity — it takes precedence over everything, instantly and without appeal. A Matriarch directs her SinCorp branch right up until the day Angora directs it otherwise, and on that day her authority simply yields. It is the one string attached to an otherwise unconditional crown: the city is hers, the laboratory is hers, the order is hers to enforce as she sees fit — until the First Matriarch decides it is not.

## Councils and Other Forms

Most mega-cities are ruled by a single Matriarch, but the title is a description of where power sits, not a law about how it must be arranged. A minority of cities are governed instead by an **oligarchy** or a **ruling council** of powerful giantesses who share authority among themselves rather than ceding it to one figure. These are the exception, not the rule — concentrated power suits the new world's temperament, and most cities trend toward a single ruler sooner or later — but they exist, and a council-ruled city can be as stable and as fearsome as any sole-Matriarch one.

Whatever the form, the same two constants bind it as bind every mega-city: the new world order is enforced, and SinCorp is present. A council that abandoned either would draw Sin's attention no less surely than a lone Matriarch who did.

## The First Matriarch

[Sin](sin) holds a place above all other Matriarchs, though she rarely troubles to exercise it. As the progenitor of the new world and ruler of [Angora](angora) — the first mega-city and unofficial capital — she is styled the **First Matriarch**, and the title is not merely ceremonial. Her authority is the one power that supersedes a Matriarch's within her own walls.

In practice Sin leaves the Matriarchs to their own cities, uninterested in the day-to-day business of ruling and content to let each do as she pleases, so long as the two constants hold. But the hierarchy is understood by all of them. A Matriarch governs at the pleasure of a First Matriarch who almost never intervenes — and whose almost-never is precisely what keeps the system in line.

## Did You Know

- A Matriarch is chosen by neither birth nor vote nor size, but by her power to command a city and to enforce the new world order within it — a standard broad enough that no two Matriarchs need have come to power the same way.
- A Matriarch can direct her city's SinCorp branch to research whatever she desires — making each city's laboratory an extension of its ruler's will, and a window into her tastes.
- The single limit on a Matriarch's authority is an order from Sin, which always takes precedence. Such orders are rare enough that many Matriarchs may rule for years without ever receiving one.
- Not every city has a Matriarch in the strict sense — a minority are ruled by councils or oligarchies of giantesses — but every city, however governed, enforces the order and houses a SinCorp branch.
- Sin is styled the **First Matriarch**, the only authority that outranks a Matriarch in her own city. She exercises it so seldom that its weight comes almost entirely from the certainty that she still can.
