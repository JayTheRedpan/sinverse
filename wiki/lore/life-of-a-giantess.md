## Overview

If [Life of a Toy](life-of-a-toy) is the world seen from the bottom, this is the same world seen from the top — and the most striking thing about it, to an outside eye, is how *ordinary* it looks. A [giantess](the-transformed) does not spend her days in constant spectacle or cruelty. She lives what is, by the measure of any settled society, a very normal modern life. It is simply a normal life underpinned, everywhere and always, by the casual fact that an entire class of people exists as her plaything.

The two things are not in tension, to her. The mundane and the dominion sit side by side without friction, because the [serum](apotheosis-serum) arranged her mind so that they would.

## An ordinary life, underpinned

Giantesses work, keep homes, have friends and partners and hobbies, and move through days that would be recognisable to anyone from the old world. Many hold **jobs** — careers, trades, positions of skill and standing. What a giantess does *not* do is anything too demeaning, because demeaning labour is precisely what [toys](toys) are for; the drudgery, the servitude, the work no one wants has an entire class assigned to it, and a giantess who did it herself would be doing a toy's job.

The same casual doubleness runs through the smallest details. A giantess may sit down to an ordinary meal of ordinary food — and may, with no more ceremony, make a meal of a shrunken toy instead, the two options sitting on the same menu without any sense of crossing a line. The abundance of the toy population (see [Reproduction & the Population](reproduction)) is what makes that casualness possible: nothing about it reads, to her, as extraordinary.

## Toys in the everyday

How a giantess treats toys is set by two things at once: her own personality, and the social norms around her.

**Her own toys** are hers to treat as she likes — as gently or as harshly as her nature runs. The one real social boundary is at the *kind* end, not the cruel one. A giantess who treats a personal toy with too much warmth — who **humanises** him, who seems to regard him as something like a peer — draws not admiration but sideways glances from her sisters, and quiet whispers of *"is she [Tempered](tempered)?"* Genuine tenderness toward a toy is the socially suspect thing, the tell that a woman's empathy might not have been rewritten as thoroughly as it should have been.

**Toys who are not hers** occupy a careful middle ground. Most giantesses will harass a passing toy as a matter of idle habit, but will largely leave one alone if he is plainly about a purpose — on a public duty, or running an errand for his owner. A toy's **collar** signals which is which: the type he wears marks his status and his business, and reads, at a glance, as *claimed and occupied* rather than *free for the taking*. That signal buys him a real measure of safety — but never a guaranteed one. The forbearance is a courtesy among giantesses, not a right held by the toy, and it can be withdrawn on any whim.

And then there are **public-use toys** — those whose whole assigned role is to be available, to serve whatever need a giantess passing by might have of them. They are the moving parts of a city's convenience, owned by no one in particular and at the disposal of anyone.

## Among her sisters

Whatever a giantess's station, the texture of her life with *other giantesses* is collegial in a way her life with toys never is. The [serum's](apotheosis-serum) rewrite points dominance only downward; between women it runs the other way, into the fierce, warm solidarity described on [The Transformed](the-transformed). Wealth, city, and rank shape a giantess's life enormously — a [Matriarch's](matriarchs) days look nothing like a [SinCorp](sincorp) researcher's or an ordinary citizen's — but the baseline holds across all of them: sisterhood above, dominion below, and between the two a life that is otherwise unremarkably, comfortably normal.

## The long ease

Underneath it all sits time. The serum nearly halted a giantess's ageing, and a life without a foreseeable end has a particular unhurried quality to it. There is no scramble, no clock, no sense of a window closing. A giantess settles into her world the way one settles into a house she never intends to leave — because, as far as anyone can tell, she never will.

## Did You Know?

- A giantess's life is, on the surface, an **ordinary modern life** — work, home, leisure — underpinned by the casual domination of a class kept as playthings.
- Giantesses hold jobs, but rarely demeaning ones: menial labour is what [toys](toys) are for.
- The same casualness runs to the table — a giantess may eat ordinary food or a shrunken toy with equal unconcern.
- The socially suspect thing is **kindness**: humanising a personal toy draws whispers of *"is she [Tempered](tempered)?"*, not cruelty.
- A toy's **collar** signals his status and errand; it buys a passing toy real but never guaranteed safety from other giantesses.
- **Public-use toys** belong to no one in particular and exist to serve whoever needs them.
- Rank and wealth change a giantess's life enormously, but sisterhood-above and dominion-below is the constant underneath all of it.
