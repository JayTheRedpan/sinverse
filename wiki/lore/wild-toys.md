## Overview

**Wild Toys** are the [toys](toys) who live outside the cities altogether — males who fled into the [Wilds](the-wilds) or were never caught in the first place, surviving in the one place the [Transformed](the-transformed) do not directly rule. The word is worn as much as it is applied: handed down from above as a label of ownership, *toy* is taken up out here as something between a name and a refusal — an acknowledgement of what the world calls them and a rejection of being reduced to it.

There is no comfort in the choice. By every practical measure the Wilds are far more dangerous than any city. But they are also the only ground on which a toy belongs to himself, and for some that trade is worth every terror in it.

## A life of survival

There is not much room for *culture* in a life spent staying alive. Wild Toy existence is, for the overwhelming majority, survival and little else — a daily calculus of food, concealment, and not being where something large is about to be. What social life exists is improvised and fragile, built in the gaps left by the country's constant, indifferent violence.

Where toys do gather, their settlements have the character of **pioneer outposts**: small, scratched-out communities trying their hardest to become self-sustaining through a mix of homesteading and salvage. They farm what the [Overgrown](the-overgrown) will let them, and they pick through the bones of the old world — its collapsed cities and buried infrastructure — for tools, materials, and the occasional working relic. Most of these settlements struggle, and many do not last: too small to defend, too exposed to hide, and raided often enough that permanence is the exception, not the rule.

## Bastion

The great exception, and the only known **long-term** settlement, is [**Bastion**](bastion) — spoken of as proof that toys can do more than merely endure out here. Because it has lasted where other settlements are scattered and short-lived, Bastion is one of the few places a genuine *culture* has had room to form: something beyond survival, with continuity, memory, and a life of its own. Whether it is quite as real or as safe as the stories claim is a question the Wild Toys are careful never to answer honestly — its safety depends on no one being certain it exists.

## Who is out there

The free population of the Wilds is **overwhelmingly male**. Bastion runs somewhat more balanced, but everywhere else the arithmetic is stark, for two reasons.

A handful of [**Tempered**](tempered) — Transformed who kept their empathy — make their homes among the free toys, but almost only in Bastion. The smaller settlements are raided far too often for a giantess-sized ally to risk living in one; Bastion's relative security is the one place the arrangement is survivable.

And there are still **untransformed women** living among the toys — but they are remarkably rare, and stay rare by force. The moment the giantesses learn of one, she is pursued with startling determination: not as an enemy but as a *sister*, to be found and "liberated" and helped, the giantesses fully believing they are rescuing her into her birthright rather than taking something from her. An untransformed woman in the Wilds is, in effect, hunted by kindness — which makes her no less hunted, and no less likely to be found.

## The hunted freedom

A Wild Toy's freedom is never a safe one. He contends with the [Overgrown](the-overgrown) in all its forms, with the [macro](macro) whose territories he must read like weather, and — most deliberately of all — with the [Huntresses](the-paws), professionals who come into the Wilds specifically to take wild-caught toys back to a market that prizes them. Freedom out here is a thing held onto in spite of everything, day by day, by toys who have decided that a hunted life of their own is worth more than a safe life of belonging to someone else.

## Did You Know?

- "Wild Toy" is both a status and a claimed identity — the word *toy*, imposed from above, is worn below as a name and a refusal.
- Wild Toy life is overwhelmingly **survival**, with little room for culture — except at [Bastion](bastion), the one long-term settlement where real community has formed.
- Smaller settlements live like **pioneer outposts**, self-sustaining through homesteading and salvage from the old world, and are raided often enough that few last.
- The free population is **vastly male**; only Bastion runs more balanced.
- A few [Tempered](tempered) live among free toys, but almost only in Bastion — smaller settlements are raided too often to risk it.
- Untransformed women still live in the Wilds but are **remarkably rare**, because giantesses pursue any they hear of relentlessly — sincerely believing they are *rescuing* a sister.
