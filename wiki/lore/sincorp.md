## Overview

SinCorp is the organization Sin built around herself — first the instrument of TDay, now the closest thing the post-TDay world has to a global institution. It began as a single bio-research laboratory and became the engine of an apocalypse; today it operates in every [mega-city](mega-cities) at once, the quiet infrastructure beneath the new order. Publicly it supplies the things the ruling class cannot do without: the tools of the hunt, the modifications that keep [toys](toys) usable, the water in the pipes. Privately it pursues whatever Sin is curious about that week.

Its stated purpose is biological research and urban services. Its actual purpose is whatever Sin decides it is — and she rarely explains.

## Founding

SinCorp did not start as Sin's. She built the Apotheosis Serum as a researcher at someone else's laboratory, working with a mind that outstripped everyone around her, and when she understood what she had — and what she intended to do with it — the lab became the one thing standing between her and the world she meant to make. So she took it.

How, exactly, she manoeuvred herself into control of the place is not documented, and the people best positioned to describe it did not remain available to be asked. Her old superiors and several colleagues simply stopped being part of the story — no announcements, no successors, no explanations. By the time the laboratory re-emerged restructured under her own name, the question of how a junior researcher had come to own it outright was no longer one anyone was raising. SinCorp existed. Sin ran it. The world had other things to worry about soon enough.

## The Quiet Years: Funding an Apocalypse

To remake the world, Sin needed money, infrastructure, and reach — and she acquired all three the way ambitious firms always have: on government contracts.

In the years before TDay, SinCorp presented itself as a mid-tier pharmaceutical and defence research company, and its flagship product was the **super-soldier serum** — a line of treatments sold to militaries promising hardier, faster-healing, more resilient troops. The programs were real. The soldiers who received them were genuinely tougher. What none of the procurement officers signing the cheques understood was what they were actually buying.

The super-soldier serums were never really about soldiers. They were the **early, crude ancestors of the toy modifications** SinCorp would later sell to the giantesses — durability, accelerated healing, enhanced resilience, all field-tested at scale on willing armies who believed they were sharpening a sword. In truth they were debugging the technology that would one day keep toys alive beneath the women who replaced them. The most direct descendant of that work is **Keepsake**, the crush-resistance modification, which traces its lineage straight back to those military contracts. The militaries of the old world paid to perfect the leash that would later be used on their own kind.

The contracts did more than fund the work. They embedded SinCorp in supply chains, water systems, logistics networks, and distribution channels across the globe — the exact infrastructure Sin would need to put the Apotheosis Serum everywhere at once. Behind the respectable façade of a defence contractor, the laboratory was quietly becoming the only organization on earth positioned to end the world in a single morning.

## The Hub of TDay

When the time came, SinCorp was the apparatus through which TDay was executed. The serum was released simultaneously through every vector the company had spent years putting in place — airborne dispersal staged as routine infrastructure work, contaminated water supplies, and direct delivery through SinCorp's own commercial and distribution networks. No single mechanism was responsible; that was the point. The company had seeded so many channels that there was no escaping the result and no clean way to reconstruct, afterward, exactly how it had been done.

It was the most consequential logistics operation in history, and it wore the face of a pharmaceutical company doing maintenance. By the time anyone grasped what SinCorp had really been building, the old world was already gone.

## After TDay: The Public Face

When the Chaos subsided and the mega-cities rose, SinCorp rose with them. Today every major mega-city hosts its own SinCorp presence, and the company has made itself indispensable to the new order through the things it openly provides.

**Urban infrastructure.** SinCorp holds the contracts for water, power, and logistics in most mega-cities. It controls the water supply of nearly every major city — a fact that is not a coincidence, and a dependency Sin has never needed to exercise openly to benefit from.

**The tools of the hunt.** The [huntresses](the-paws) who range the mega-cities and [the Wilds](the-wilds) rely on SinCorp's catalogue of capture equipment — the feralizers, pheromone weapons, shrink technology, and other novelty armaments developed to add sport to running down toys. Useless old-world weapons were replaced by an entire industry of purpose-built ones, and SinCorp supplies it.

**Toy modifications.** The single most visible pillar of SinCorp's public business: the [catalogue of enhancements](toy-modifications) that make toys able to survive, serve, and satisfy partners many times their size. Bottomless, Keepsake, Second Wind, the whole product line — these are SinCorp's, sold openly to any giantess who can pay, and the practical reason the size gap that defines the new world is survivable at all.

**[Reproduction](reproduction).** With female reproductive anatomy eliminated by the serum, and the toy population having collapsed after TDay before SinCorp's vat-growing stabilized it, that same technology is what keeps the world populated at all — tube-grown toys that hold the supply steady against bottomless demand, occasionally new girls, and the modifications that let toys themselves carry pregnancies. The continuation of the species runs through SinCorp's laboratories.

## After TDay: The Hidden Work

Everything above is the part SinCorp will admit to. It is not the part that matters most.

Behind the infrastructure contracts and the product catalogue, every mega-city's SinCorp laboratory pursues its own branch of research, and the nature of that research is not disclosed to anyone — not the [Matriarchs](matriarchs), not the public, often not other SinCorp divisions. The visible business of water and weapons and modifications is, in large part, the cover and the funding stream for whatever Sin actually wants to know.

The flagship of that hidden work is [**Project Sylph**](project-sylph) — Sin's personal initiative to engineer an entirely new class of being between the giantesses and the toys, using a serum reworked to key off femininity rather than womanhood. Sylph is only the program the world has caught a glimpse of. The rest remains behind doors that do not appear on any city's filed blueprints. SinCorp's experiments are the source of most of the strange, the unexplained, and the genuinely dangerous in the post-TDay world; when something inexplicable surfaces, SinCorp research is very often the answer, even when no one can prove it.

## Structure

SinCorp operates through a network of regional divisions, each nominally reporting to a central body in Angora but in practice running with significant autonomy — regional directors interpret Sin's broad directives and execute them as they see fit, so long as results arrive. Promotion is fast for those who produce. The consequences of failure are not advertised, and do not need to be.

The company has no public-facing leadership other than Sin herself. There are no press releases, no earnings calls, no external board. It simply operates, and the world accommodates it. It employs more [Transformed](the-transformed) researchers than any other organization in existence, alongside a significant number of toys in administrative and service roles — who are never permitted inside the research facilities.

## Relationship to the Mega-Cities

Each mega-city Matriarch maintains nominal independence from SinCorp and from Angora. In practice, SinCorp's infrastructure contracts create dependencies that hand it quiet leverage over every city that relies on them — which is to say, all of them. Sin does not use that leverage openly. She does not need to. A city that gets its water, its weapons, and its toy modifications from a single source is already governed by it in every way that counts.

## Did You Know

- SinCorp did not begin as Sin's company — she took control of the laboratory where she developed the serum, and the people who might have contested that takeover did not remain available to contest it.
- Before TDay, SinCorp's flagship product was a line of military **super-soldier serums**. They were never really about soldiers — they were the precursors to toy modifications, field-tested on armies who believed they were buying durability. **Keepsake** descends directly from that work.
- The government contracts did more than fund SinCorp. They embedded the company in the water systems, supply chains, and distribution networks it would later use to deliver TDay everywhere at once.
- SinCorp controls the water supply of nearly every major mega-city. This is not a coincidence.
- Every mega-city's SinCorp laboratory runs its own undisclosed research. The public business of infrastructure and modifications largely funds and conceals the work Sin actually cares about.
- **Project Sylph** is only the hidden program the world has glimpsed. SinCorp's central research complex in Angora is the largest structure in the city — and the only one whose blueprints are filed with no city authority.
- At least three Resistance operations have been mounted against SinCorp facilities. None has resulted in any public disclosure of what those facilities contained.
