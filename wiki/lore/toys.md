## Overview

*Toy* is the term used to refer to males in the post-TDay world. It is derogatory in origin, applied by the Transformed in the aftermath of TDay to describe a population they no longer regarded as peers. It has since become the universal shorthand — used by the Transformed casually, by toys themselves with varying degrees of acceptance or bitterness, and by the Resistance as a category of people worth protecting.

Males are not citizens in the post-TDay world. They hold no formal status, no rights, and no protections under any mega-city governance structure. What they experience depends entirely on who owns them, which city they are in, and how much luck they have encountered along the way.

## Categories

**City toys.** The majority of the toy population lives within the mega-cities. Their situations vary: domestic servants, entertainment, involuntary research subjects. The culture of the individual city and its Matriarch determine what is and is not done to them. Some are treated with relative care. Most are not.

**Wild Toys.** Males who have escaped into the Wilds and built lives outside city authority. Survival in the Wilds is difficult; wild toys who make it tend to be resourceful, cautious, and connected to informal networks of others like them.

**Resistance-affiliated toys.** Some toys work actively with the Resistance to extract others from the cities. This is among the most dangerous occupations in the Sinverse world.

## The Word Itself

The choice to use *toy* rather than any neutral term is not accidental. It reflects exactly how the Transformed majority regards males — as objects that exist for use, entertainment, or disposal. The Resistance makes a point of using the word with reclaimed dignity. SinCorp uses it in clinical documentation without apparent irony.

Jay is a toy.

## Did You Know

- The word "toy" entered common usage within the first two weeks after TDay. No one has been credited with originating it.
- SinCorp's internal documentation refers to toys using a clinical alphanumeric designation system. The word "toy" appears in the informal notes of SinCorp researchers but not in official records.
- The Resistance has documented that toys who maintain connections to other toys — family, friendship, community — have significantly higher survival rates in the Wilds than those who operate alone.
- A small number of Matriarchs across the mega-cities have extended limited protections to toys within their cities. None have extended citizenship. The distinction matters enormously.
- The toy population globally is estimated to have declined by 60–70% in the five years since TDay. Demographic models suggest continued decline. The Resistance considers this the core urgency of their work.
