## Overview

**The Pack** is a gang of [giantesses](the-transformed), unusual in that every one of its members is a **canine** — dobermans, collies, and their kin, bound together as much by a shared breed-pride as by the turf they hold. Where most of the new world's power is organised into cities, corporations, and formal factions, the Pack is something older and rougher: a territorial gang that rules a stretch of ruin by presence and reputation, and answers to no one above it.

They are led by **[Hailey](hailey)**, and they carry themselves according to a simple creed — that dogs are a superior breed, born to rule, and that everything smaller exists for their use and amusement.

## Territory

The Pack claims the **abandoned edges of a mega-city** — the old slums and stalled construction sites that the city never got around to redeveloping. It is exactly the kind of ground the ordinary order overlooks: too ruined to be valuable, too marginal to be patrolled, and mistaken by the unwary for somewhere *safe* precisely because the "big girls" supposedly have no reason to be there.

That mistake is the Pack's bread and butter. The ruins are their den and their hunting ground both, and the whole district is **marked as their territory** — scent-staked in the plain, animal way, a warning that anything with a keener nose would read from a distance. [Toys](toys) who slip into the district looking for a quiet place to lie low tend to discover, too late, that they have walked into the middle of a pack's range.

## The Pack and its ways

For all their menace, the Pack are not an army or an institution; they are a **gang** — a loose, boisterous sisterhood held together by common breed and common appetite rather than any chain of command. Members come and go around a shared den, keep in contact, gather for the hunt, and treat the whole thing with the rowdy, informal energy of a crew that genuinely enjoys each other's company. There is talk of drinking parties as readily as of hunting.

Their canine nature shapes everything about how they operate:

- **The nose leads.** The Pack hunts and holds territory by scent, tracking prey and marking ground the way their breed always has. A toy's poor sense of smell is a running joke among them — he cannot even tell when he has wandered somewhere he shouldn't.
- **Breed-supremacy is the creed.** The Pack's members believe, sincerely and loudly, that canines are the finest of the Transformed — the breed *born to rule* — and much of their cruelty toward a captured toy is framed as "teaching it its place beneath the dogs."
- **Everything is shared.** A catch belongs to the Pack, not the individual. When one member takes a toy, the others are told, and they come around as they please. A single unlucky toy can find himself the whole gang's plaything for as long as their interest holds.

## Place in the world

The Pack is a small, local power — one gang holding one district — rather than a faction of any real reach. But they are a vivid example of a particular corner of the new world: the **giantesses who never bothered with the cities' order at all**, and simply carved out territory to rule on their own terms. They keep no toys as property in the settled, domestic sense; they take them from their turf, use them among the group, and think no further ahead than that.

To the mega-city beside them, the Pack is a piece of ambient danger — a reason the old slums stay abandoned, and a story told to warn the reckless away from the ruins. To a toy, they are one of the many ways that a place that looks empty and safe turns out to be neither.

## Did You Know?

- Every member of the Pack is a **canine** giantess — the shared breed is the gang's whole identity, not a coincidence.
- They hold the **abandoned slums and ruins** on a mega-city's edge — ground people assume is safe *because* they assume no giantess would bother with it.
- The Pack marks its entire territory by scent, and finds it endlessly funny that a [toy's](toys) dull nose can't read the warning.
- A toy caught by one member becomes the **whole gang's** — catches are shared, and word travels fast through the pack.
- Their guiding belief is that **dogs are a superior breed, born to rule** — a breed-pride that colours everything they do.
- The Pack is led by **[Hailey](hailey)**, who by her own admission is not even the largest of them.
