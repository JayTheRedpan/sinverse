## Overview

Angora is the first mega-city, the most powerful, and the unofficial capital of the post-TDay world. It is the seat of [Sin's](sin) authority, the global headquarters of [SinCorp](sincorp), and the standard against which every other [mega-city](mega-cities) measures itself — in scale, in influence, and in the particular, total culture of power that defines life within its walls. If the new world has a centre, it is here. Where Sin is, the world turns.

It is also, in a sense the other cities are not, a pure thing. Most mega-cities grew — adapted, improvised, shaped by whoever seized them. Angora was *designed*, from nothing, to be exactly what its maker intended. It is the new world order rendered without compromise: the clearest statement anywhere of what Sin built TDay to create.

## The Designed City

Angora was not rebuilt from an old-world city. It was raised from open ground that had held no major settlement before, on a site Sin chose precisely because it came with nothing to work around — no existing streets to widen, no buildings to gut, no compromises baked into the bones of someone else's world. A blank slate, for a blank-slate civilisation.

The result is the most liveable environment the Transformed have. Where cities adapted from pre-TDay architecture are forever making do — doors enlarged, ceilings raised, old human proportions showing through the renovations — Angora was built giantess-scale from its first foundation. Its grid accommodates inhabitants up to thirty feet comfortably and as a matter of course; the merely tall move through it without thought. Anything larger than the city's baseline relies on specialist access routes, the one concession its design makes to the upper extremes of scale.

Beneath the gleaming order of its upper city — the broad thoroughfares, the towering residences of the Transformed, the plazas built for beings the size of buildings — Angora runs the same machinery every mega-city does, simply more completely: the markets and management of toys, the infrastructure of water and power and logistics that SinCorp threads through everything, and, at its heart, the laboratories. Like every city it is an island in the [Wilds](the-wilds), and as the capital it is the most secure island of them all.

## The Seat of the New Order

Angora is where the new world order is most fully realised — and, paradoxically, where its architect is least interested in ruling.

Sin governs Angora, but not in any way a visitor would recognise as governance. There are no public appearances, no proclamations, no court. There is an administrative apparatus, and it runs the city's day-to-day with real competence — but everyone within it understands that they are *implementing a vision*, not exercising authority of their own. Sin sets the shape of the thing and leaves the execution to others, because the business of running a city bores her and her attention is almost always elsewhere, down in the work. The strange result is that Angora, the seat of the most powerful being alive, is a city governed largely in her absence — a monument run by people carrying out the will of someone who would rather be in a laboratory.

And yet her hand is everywhere in it, precisely because Angora reflects her vision rather than her daily rule. Other Matriarchs actively govern their cities, and those cities take on the texture of active rulers. Angora takes on the texture of Sin's *intent* — the purest, most uncompromised expression of the order she designed the whole world to hold. It is the model the order is supposed to look like, maintained in her image whether she is paying attention to it or not.

## The Heart of SinCorp

Angora is the global headquarters of [SinCorp](sincorp), and that — more than any throne — is what makes it the centre of the world.

Every mega-city houses a SinCorp branch, but they all answer, ultimately, to the work done here. Angora holds the company's central research complex: the single largest structure in the city, and the only building whose blueprints are filed with no administrative body at all. It is where SinCorp's deepest and most closely held research is done, [Project Sylph](project-sylph) among it, and it is where Sin herself actually spends her time — not ruling, but creating. The capital's true heart is not a seat of government but a laboratory, and the woman at its centre is a researcher first and a ruler a distant second.

It is this, finally, that the other cities measure themselves against. Angora's power is not that Sin sits atop it; it is that the engine of all the new world's science, transformation, and possibility runs out of its core.

## Life in Angora

To live in Angora as one of the Transformed is to live at the centre of the world, in the most complete version of the order anywhere. The city's culture is one of total, settled power — not the scramble of a frontier or the improvisation of an adapted city, but the unhurried confidence of a place that has everything it intends to have and intends to keep it. As everywhere, that society is a sisterhood: giantesses in Angora cooperate, bond, and share the city as peers, with none of the rivalry or one-upmanship the old world's powerful would have fallen into. The dominance that defines Angora points only ever downward, at the toys; among the women there is solidarity, not competition.

For a toy, Angora is the order at its most absolute. This is Sin's own city, where the principle that toys are livestock is not a regional cruelty but the foundational fact of the place, maintained personally and deliberately by the being who invented it. And over all of it hangs the particular dread of Sin's proximity: to be in Angora is to be in her city, and to be summoned to her is a thing from which toys are, as a rule, simply never seen to return.

## The First City

Angora's place above the other mega-cities is real but rarely exercised. It is the unofficial capital, the first city and the most powerful, and its Matriarch is [Sin](sin) herself — styled the [First Matriarch](matriarchs), the one authority whose word overrides any other Matriarch within her own walls.

In practice the cities are independent nations, and Angora does not rule them. Other Matriarchs keep their full autonomy and their distance, and there is no rivalry in the relationship — only the same easy mutual respect that holds between all the mega-cities, sharpened here by what Angora represents. None of them is insensible to it. Angora is the city where the order began, where SinCorp's heart beats, and where the First Matriarch resides; a Matriarch may rule her own city as she pleases, but she does so in a world whose centre, and whose final authority, is here. That the First Matriarch almost never exercises that authority is exactly what makes its weight so quietly absolute.

## Did You Know

- Angora was built on land that previously held no major city. Sin chose the site specifically because it came with no pre-existing architecture to compromise her vision.
- The name Angora was chosen by Sin, who has never explained it. Many note that *angora* is a breed of rabbit, and that Sin is a black bunny — but she has never confirmed the connection, and asking has never been advisable.
- Angora was designed giantess-scale from its first foundation, making it markedly more liveable for the Transformed than cities adapted from old-world architecture. Its grid comfortably accommodates inhabitants up to thirty feet; anything larger requires specialist access routes.
- Sin does not rule Angora through visible governance. Its administrative apparatus understands itself to be implementing her vision, not exercising authority of its own — which makes the capital a city run, in effect, in its ruler's absence.
- SinCorp's central research complex in Angora is the largest structure in the city, and the only building whose blueprints are filed with no administrative body. It is where Sin actually spends her time.
- Angora has never officially published its population figures — but it is easily the largest of the mega-cities.
