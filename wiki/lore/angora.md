## Overview

Angora is the most powerful mega-city in the post-TDay world, and the seat of Sin's personal authority. It serves as the headquarters of SinCorp and the standard against which other mega-cities measure themselves — in infrastructure, in influence, and in the particular culture of power that defines life within its borders.

## Character

The specifics of Angora's districts, culture, and geography are still being documented. What is known:

Angora was not rebuilt from an existing city — it was constructed. The infrastructure was designed from inception for giantess-scale inhabitants, making it one of the more liveable environments for the Transformed compared to mega-cities adapted from pre-TDay architecture.

Sin does not rule Angora through visible governance. There is an administrative apparatus, but those within it understand they are implementing a vision, not exercising independent authority.

## Angora and the Other Cities

Each mega-city in the Sinverse world is ruled by a Matriarch who sets its culture and laws. Angora's Matriarch is Sin herself, which gives it a particular status — other Matriarchs maintain independence from Angora politically, but none are insensible to what it represents.
