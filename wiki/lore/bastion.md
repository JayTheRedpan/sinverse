## Overview

Bastion is the city that may not exist.

It is spoken of, everywhere and always, as a rumour — a story the [Wild Toys](the-wilds) tell each other in the dark, a hope passed hand to hand down the length of the [Resistance's](the-resistance) underground railroad. The rumour says there is a place, hidden deep in the [Wilds](the-wilds), where toys do not merely survive but *live* — a permanent settlement, generations deep, where unaltered women still walk free and no giantess has ever set her shadow. The rumour says it has lasted where every other toy haven has fallen. The rumour says its name is Bastion.

The remarkable thing about the rumour is that it is true. The more remarkable thing is that even the people who *know* it is true still speak of it as a rumour — on purpose, without exception, because the moment Bastion becomes a fact instead of a story is the moment it dies.

## A Rumour Kept Alive

Bastion's first and last line of defence is disbelief. To the mega-cities and the [Paws](the-paws) who range the Wilds, a permanent toy city would be the ultimate prize — a single haul worth more than a lifetime of solitary catches, and a target the whole apparatus of the new world would mobilise to crush. A confirmed location would not survive the season.

So Bastion is protected by a discipline as much as a place: everyone who knows of it lies about it. Those who have been there speak of it as a thing they once heard of. Those who guide others toward it never name a destination, only a next step. Even within the Resistance, knowledge of Bastion is fractured deliberately, so that no single captured operative can give it up because no single operative holds the whole of it. The result is that Bastion lives in a permanent fog of its own making — real to those who reach it, deniable to everyone else, and impossible to confirm from the outside. It is the best-kept secret in a world built on cruelty, and it is kept by the simple, universal agreement never to admit it is kept at all.

## Where It Is

No one outside Bastion can say where Bastion is, and those inside it do not tell.

The rumours disagree, which is its own kind of armour. Some say it lies deep underground, a buried city beneath the roots of the monstrous canopy, sealed away from anything large enough to threaten it. Some say it sits in a region of the Wilds so dense, so dark, or so dangerous that no huntress would follow a trail into it. Some say it is shielded, hidden, or simply lost on purpose — tucked into one of the blank zones even the Resistance avoids. Which of these is true, if any single one is, has never been established, and Bastion's people are content to let every version circulate. A secret with one story can be cracked. A secret with a dozen contradictory ones cannot even be approached.

## Who Lives There

Bastion is a true settlement, not a hideout — and the proof of that is who calls it home.

**Toys**, first and most: men living not as property, not as prey, but as people, in numbers and permanence no scattered Wild village has ever managed. Bastion is the standing argument that a toy's life can be more than survival between catches.

**Unaltered women.** Most strikingly, Bastion is home to women the serum never took — those rare females who escaped transformation and live here free, among the toys, as neighbours and partners and family rather than overlords. Their presence is part of what makes Bastion a *city* rather than a refuge: it is a place where ordinary life, the kind the old world had, persists in defiance of everything outside.

**The Tempered.** And, more uneasily, some of the [Tempered](the-resistance) live in Bastion as well — transformed women who kept their empathy and want no part of the new order. Bastion is one of the only places on earth where a Tempered woman can live openly among toys rather than hiding what she is. But she is never entirely trusted. To many in Bastion a Tempered is a kindness with a fuse on it — a giantess who has, so far, never given in to the appetites the serum built into her, living among the very beings those appetites are aimed at. Most never do give in. The unease that they *might* never fully leaves the room, and the Tempered who make their homes in Bastion carry the weight of being loved and watched in the same breath.

## A Place Where Tenderness Is Possible

For all that wariness, Bastion is the one place in the Sinverse where something other than horror can grow. It is the only setting in the world where a toy and a partner can build a life together — where an untransformed woman and a toy can simply *be* a couple, where a careful, hard-won romance with a Tempered woman is even thinkable, where there are homes and families and futures rather than only the next escape. The softer stories the new world otherwise forbids have exactly one place they can happen, and it is here.

That is precisely what makes Bastion so potent, and so fragile. It is the spark of hope in a dark world — and a spark is a thing the world is always reaching to snuff out. Everything tender that Bastion makes possible is shadowed by what waits outside its secret and, sometimes, by what waits inside the heart of a Tempered woman who has held on so far. Bastion is not safety. It is hope held one careful day at a time, and that is a far more precious and far more dangerous thing.

## The End of the Railroad

Bastion is not a Resistance city — it governs itself, an independent settlement answerable to no one outside its own walls. But it is the destination the Resistance's underground railroad ultimately reaches toward. The cells that smuggle toys out of the mega-cities and through the Wilds are moving them, in the end, toward the one place a freed toy can actually go and stay free. The Resistance routes to Bastion; it does not run it.

Bastion is also believed to be the seed of much of the free Wilds. The persistent pattern of new toy villages appearing across the country is rumoured to trace back here — groups setting out from Bastion to found settlements of their own, carrying its proof-of-concept outward. If true, it makes Bastion not merely a refuge but a wellspring: the origin from which the fragile, scattered hope of the Wilds keeps renewing itself.

## The Ultimate Goal

Whether or not a toy has ever heard the name, Bastion is what every toy is reaching for — the place where the running stops. It is the answer to the question the new world is built to make unanswerable: *is there anywhere a toy can simply live?* Bastion says yes. Quietly, deniably, never quite confirmed — but yes.

That is the whole of its meaning and the whole of its danger. As long as Bastion is a rumour, it is the hope that keeps the Wild Toys moving and the Resistance working. The day it becomes a known place on a map is the day the new world comes for it. And so it remains exactly what it has always been: the city that may not exist, and had better not, for as long as it possibly can.

## Did You Know

- Bastion is real — and everyone who knows it is real still calls it a rumour on purpose, because the secret is its single most important defence.
- It is the only proven permanent toy city in the world. Every other toy haven has been temporary; Bastion has lasted generations.
- No two accounts of Bastion's location agree. Some say it is underground, some say it is hidden in the deep Wilds, some say it lies in a blank zone — and its people are happy to let every version circulate.
- Unaltered women — females the serum never transformed — live freely in Bastion among the toys, which is much of what makes it a true city rather than a hideout.
- Some Tempered live in Bastion too, openly, in one of the only places on earth they can. They are loved and watched in equal measure — never quite trusted not to one day become what the serum intended.
- Bastion is independent. The Resistance routes its underground railroad toward Bastion but does not govern it.
- Many of the new toy villages that appear across the Wilds are rumoured to have been founded by groups setting out from Bastion — making it the possible wellspring of the free Wilds.
- To a huntress, a confirmed Bastion would be the single greatest catch imaginable. This is exactly why it must never be confirmed.
