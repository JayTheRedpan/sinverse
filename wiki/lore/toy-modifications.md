# SinCorp Toy Modifications

As the giantesses grew, so did the gap between what they wanted and what an ordinary toy could survive. A partner thirty, sixty, a hundred times your size is not a danger you can simply be careful around — and so **SinCorp** built a business out of closing that gap. Its catalogue of **Toy Modifications** is the quiet engineering that makes the new world *function*: the enhancements a giantess can have installed in a toy so that he can better serve, endure, and please the ruling class.

Marketed with SinCorp's trademark blend of the clinical and the suggestive, each modification is sold as an upgrade to the toy and a convenience to his owner. Together they form the technical backbone of toy ownership in the post-TDay world.

## The catalogue

### Accommodation — the *Cavern Line*

The most requested modification of all. The **Cavern Line** re-engineers a toy's orifices to **stretch to extraordinary sizes** and return unharmed, accommodating anatomy that would otherwise be simply impossible to take. Without it, the size gap that defines the new world would make most pairings unworkable; with it, there is effectively no overlord a toy cannot be made to fit.

Owners often refer to a fully-treated toy as **"bottomless,"** a piece of slang SinCorp has happily adopted into its own marketing.

### Durability — *AegisFlesh*

**AegisFlesh** dramatically increases a toy's structural resilience, letting him **withstand crushing forces** that would pulp an unmodified body — the incidental weight of a giantess shifting in her sleep, the grip of a hand many times his size, the simple physics of being beneath someone enormous. It is the difference between a toy who survives his owner's enthusiasm and one who does not.

AegisFlesh holds a special place in SinCorp's history: it is the **direct descendant of the company's pre-TDay "super-soldier" work** (see *Origins*, below).

### Regeneration — the *Phoenix Treatment*

Accidents happen, especially at scale. The **Phoenix Treatment** vastly accelerates a toy's healing, knitting injuries closed in a fraction of the natural time so he can **return to his duties quickly** rather than being set aside to mend. Paired with AegisFlesh, it makes a toy not merely durable but very nearly **disposable-proof** — able to take damage that would end an ordinary body and be back in service by morning.

### Resizing — *ScaleShift*

It is well established that the new world's women routinely **shrink their toys**, reducing them to the size of micros for amusement, portability, punishment, or simple preference. **ScaleShift** is the SinCorp resizing platform that makes this safe and reversible, letting an owner dial a toy down to a few inches and back at will. A micro'd toy fits in a palm, a pocket, or a mouth — and ScaleShift ensures he survives the journey in either direction.

### Fertility — the *Cradle Chip*

A small implant with enormous implications: the **Cradle Chip** reconfigures a toy's body so that he can **be impregnated and carry to term**. Sought after by giantesses who want offspring on their own terms, it is among SinCorp's most intimate and most controversial products — and a quiet cornerstone of how certain bloodlines now propagate in the new world.

### Pliancy — the *Docility Suite* ("petting")

The **Docility Suite** dampens a toy's higher mental function, trading wit and will for an easy, contented pliability — turning a person into something closer to a **devoted pet**. Owners who want a companion rather than a conversationalist favour it; the slang for a toy treated this way is **"petted."** It is simple, it is permanent by default, and it is one of the products the **Resistance** points to most often when it speaks of what SinCorp truly sells.

### Respiration — *DeepLung*

**DeepLung** vastly increases a toy's lung capacity and tolerance for low oxygen, letting him **endure extended periods without air** — buried in cleavage, held underwater, sealed away in a fist or a mouth or somewhere more intimate, for far longer than a normal body could bear. For partners whose idea of play routinely cuts off a toy's air, it is less a luxury than a necessity.

## Other modifications

SinCorp's catalogue runs deeper than its headline products. Among the more common supplementary modifications:

- **The *Honeyed Tongue*** — alters a toy's body chemistry so that his taste and scent are pleasing at giantess scale, where an unmodified toy would simply be overwhelming.
- **ThermaCore** — lets a toy regulate against the heat and pressure of being fully enclosed against a body many times his size, a frequent companion purchase to DeepLung.
- **EverReady** — suppresses a toy's own limits and refractory period, keeping him able to perform on demand for as long as his owner wishes rather than as long as his body would naturally allow.
- **The *Leash*** — a tracking-and-recall implant that lets an owner always locate a toy, particularly valued for micro'd toys who are easily misplaced and Wild Toys prone to running.
- **SilkSkin** — a cosmetic line that renders a toy's skin flawless and pleasingly soft to the touch at scale, often bundled with the Cavern Line as a "presentation" package.

## Origins: from super-soldiers to toys

SinCorp did not begin by selling these. Before TDay — before the serum, before the new order — **Sin funded the company the way many ambitious firms do: on government contracts.** SinCorp's pre-TDay reputation was built on supplying the military with a line of **"super-soldier serums"** promising hardier, faster-healing, more resilient troops.

In truth, those serums were **precursors to the modifications catalogued here** — early, cruder versions of the same science, most directly the ancestor of **AegisFlesh**. The military believed it was buying battlefield durability. It was, without knowing it, **field-testing the technology that would one day keep toys alive beneath the giantesses.** By the time anyone understood what SinCorp had really been building, the company was already rich, already entrenched, and already most of the way to the world it intended to make.

## Did You Know?

- **AegisFlesh** descends directly from the pre-TDay **super-soldier serums** Sin sold to the military, making it arguably the oldest technology in the catalogue.
- A toy treated with the full **Cavern Line** is marketed as **"bottomless"**; one given the **Docility Suite** is said to be **"petted."**
- The pre-TDay military thought it was buying soldiers. It was, unknowingly, **funding the toy trade.**
