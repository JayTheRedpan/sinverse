## Overview

As the giantesses grew, so did the gap between what they wanted and what an ordinary toy could survive. A partner thirty, sixty, a hundred times your size is not a danger you can simply be careful around — and so **SinCorp** built a business out of closing that gap. Its catalogue of **Toy Modifications** is the quiet engineering that makes the new world *function*: the enhancements a giantess can have installed in a toy so that he can better serve, endure, and please the ruling class.

Marketed with SinCorp's trademark blend of the clinical and the suggestive, each modification carries a brisk product name on the order form and a far more telling one on the lips of the women who buy them. Together they form the technical backbone of toy ownership in the post-TDay world.

## The catalogue

### Accommodation — *Bottomless*

The most requested modification of all. **Bottomless** re-engineers a toy's orifices to **stretch to extraordinary sizes** and return unharmed, accommodating anatomy that would otherwise be simply impossible to take. Without it, the size gap that defines the new world would make most pairings unworkable; with it, there is effectively no overlord a toy cannot be made to fit. The name is also the owner's promise and the toy's sentence: there is no longer a limit to what he can be asked to take.

### Durability — *Keepsake*

**Keepsake** dramatically increases a toy's structural resilience, letting him **withstand crushing forces** that would pulp an unmodified body — the incidental weight of a giantess shifting in her sleep, the squeeze of a hand many times his size, the simple physics of being beneath someone enormous. The name is fondly possessive: a Keepsake toy is one she gets to *keep*, no matter how carelessly she handles him.

Keepsake holds a special place in SinCorp's history: it is the **direct descendant of the company's pre-TDay "super-soldier" work** (see *Origins*, below).

### Regeneration — *Second Wind*

Accidents happen, especially at scale. **Second Wind** vastly accelerates a toy's healing, knitting injuries closed in a fraction of the natural time so he can **return to his duties quickly** rather than being set aside to mend. Paired with Keepsake, it makes a toy not merely durable but very nearly tireless — able to take damage that would end an ordinary body and be back in service by morning, ready for whatever she has in mind next.

### Resizing — *Pocket Plaything*

It is well established that the new world's women routinely **shrink their toys**, reducing them to the size of micros for amusement, portability, punishment, or simple preference. **Pocket Plaything** is the SinCorp resizing platform that makes this safe and reversible, letting an owner dial a toy down to a few inches and back at will. The name says it plainly: a treated toy is small enough to carry in a pocket, and changed as easily as one — a thing to be sized to her whim and spent however she likes.

### Fertility — *Bitch Chip*

A small implant with enormous implications: **Bitch Chip** reconfigures a toy's body so that he can **be impregnated and carry to term**. Sought after by giantesses who want offspring on their own terms, it is among SinCorp's most intimate and most controversial products — and a quiet cornerstone of how certain bloodlines now propagate in the new world. The name is sold as sentiment; it is also a statement of exactly what the toy is now *for*.

### Pliancy — *Good Boy Brained*

**Good Boy Brained** dampens a toy's higher mental function, trading wit and will for an easy, contented pliability — turning a person into something closer to a **devoted pet**. Owners who want a companion rather than a conversationalist favour it, and the name is the whole appeal: it is the praise such a toy lives to hear, and very nearly the only thought he has room left to chase. It is one of the products the **Resistance** points to most often when it speaks of what SinCorp truly sells.

### Respiration — *Last Gasp*

**Last Gasp** vastly increases a toy's lung capacity and tolerance for low oxygen, letting him **endure extended periods without air** — buried in cleavage, held under, sealed away in a fist or a mouth or somewhere more intimate, for far longer than a normal body could bear. For partners whose idea of play routinely cuts off a toy's air, it is less a luxury than a necessity, and the name is a small cruelty in itself: with Last Gasp, the gasp she is waiting for simply never has to come.

## Other modifications

SinCorp's catalogue runs deeper than its headline products. Among the more common supplementary modifications:

- ***Sweet Nothing*** — alters a toy's body chemistry so that his taste and scent turn pleasing at giantess scale, where an unmodified toy would simply be overwhelming.
- ***Hot House*** — lets a toy regulate against the heat and pressure of being fully enclosed against a body many times his size; a frequent companion purchase to Last Gasp.
- ***Encore*** — suppresses a toy's own limits and refractory period, keeping him able to perform on demand for as long as his owner wishes rather than as long as his body would naturally allow.
- ***Heel*** — a tracking-and-recall implant that lets an owner always locate a toy; particularly valued for micro'd toys who are easily misplaced and Wild Toys prone to running. The name doubles as the command.
- ***Doll Parts*** — a cosmetic line that renders a toy's skin flawless and pleasingly soft at scale, often bundled with *Bottomless* as a "presentation" package.

## Origins: from super-soldiers to toys

SinCorp did not begin by selling these. Before TDay — before the serum, before the new order — **Sin funded the company the way many ambitious firms do: on government contracts.** SinCorp's pre-TDay reputation was built on supplying the military with a line of **"super-soldier serums"** promising hardier, faster-healing, more resilient troops.

In truth, those serums were **precursors to the modifications catalogued here** — early, cruder versions of the same science, most directly the ancestor of **Keepsake**. The military believed it was buying battlefield durability. It was, without knowing it, **field-testing the technology that would one day keep toys alive beneath the giantesses.** By the time anyone understood what SinCorp had really been building, the company was already rich, already entrenched, and already most of the way to the world it intended to make.

## Did You Know

- **Keepsake** descends directly from the pre-TDay **super-soldier serums** Sin sold to the military, making it arguably the oldest technology in the catalogue.
- The pre-TDay military thought it was buying soldiers. It was, unknowingly, **funding the toy trade.**
- *Good Boy Brained* is the modification the Resistance cites most often as proof of what SinCorp considers a "product."
- *Bottomless* and *Doll Parts* are frequently sold together as a single "presentation" package.
