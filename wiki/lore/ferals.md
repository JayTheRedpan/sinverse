## Overview

The **Ferals** are the [serum's](apotheosis-serum) cruelest outcome: [Transformed](the-transformed) whose minds it did not harden or reshape but **destroyed**, leaving instinct where a person used to be. A Feral has no reason, no self, and no persona — only appetite and reflex running a large and dangerous body.

Crucially, a Feral is not defined by size. Feralization is a **creation outlier** — a rare, random failure of the transformation itself, much like the [Tempered](tempered) are an outlier at the other extreme. It has nothing to do with how large a woman grew or anything she did. Most Ferals are no bigger than an ordinary [giantess](the-transformed), commonly in the **12-to-15-foot range**, though the outlier can strike at any size at all.

## A creation outlier

Where the [Tempered](tempered) come through transformation with their empathy intact against every intention of the [serum](apotheosis-serum), the Ferals come through it with nothing intact at all. Both are the same kind of thing: not a consequence, not a punishment, not a stage a woman passes through, but a **roll of the dice at the moment of transformation** that lands, rarely, on ruin. For reasons no one has explained, the serum now and then destroys the mind it was meant to remake, and the result is a Feral.

Because it is an outlier of *creation* rather than of scale, a Feral is usually an unremarkable size — which is part of what makes them so unsettling. One does not need to stray into the territory of a hundred-foot [macro](macro) to meet a mind that is simply gone.

## More common in humans

The outlier does not fall evenly across the population. Feralization is observed to strike the **human** minority who receive the [serum](apotheosis-serum) markedly more often than it strikes anthros — for what underlying reason, no one has established, though it is a consistent enough pattern that it is treated as fact.

Stranger still, feralization in humans is not always only a change of mind. In a number of cases the failure runs so deep that it drags the *body* with it: some human recipients emerge not merely mindless but **anthro**, their human form reshaped into a beast's as the transformation loses its grip on them entirely. It is as though, in losing hold of the mind, the serum loses hold of the shape as well — and what walks away is neither the woman who went in nor anything that could be called human afterward.

## What a Feral is

A Feral is driven by instinct alone, with nothing behind her eyes to reason with, appeal to, or bargain against. She holds no territory in any deliberate sense, keeps no company, and answers to nothing but the moment's impulse. She is a hazard rather than a resident of anywhere — something to be avoided, never engaged.

She is not a monster in the way the [Overgrown](the-overgrown) are monsters. The Overgrown were never minds at all — animals and plants swelled by the [TDay Variant](apotheosis-serum). A Feral *was* a mind, and a person, and is now simply gone. That is the whole horror of the word.

## Ferals, macro, and the blurred edge

A Feral is not a [macro](macro). Feralization is a failure at creation; the mental toll of macro scale is a separate matter — the "variance problem," in which a mind is degraded or dramatically *remade* by the sheer strain of extreme size, but very often remains present. The two should not be confused: most Ferals are ordinary-sized, and most macro are not Ferals.

The edge between them can nonetheless blur at the extremes. A macro whose mind is degraded far enough by scale can arrive at an emptiness that looks, from the outside, much like a Feral's — a mind lost by a different road. Telling exactly which one is in front of you is rarely possible from a safe distance, and rarely worth the risk of finding out.

## In the Wilds

Ferals live in the [Wilds](the-wilds), and are counted among its dangers — one more threat a [Wild Toy](wild-toys) must survive, alongside the [macro](macro) whose movements he tracks like weather and the [Overgrown](the-overgrown) that fill the country. What sets a Feral apart is that she offers *nothing to read*: no persona, no pattern, no logic of territory, only the certainty that being noticed by one is very likely the end. [SinCorp](sincorp) marks the ground a Feral roams as **off-limits even to its own people** — a rare admission of danger from an organisation not much given to caution. For anyone small, the only sound course is never to be where a Feral is.

## The tragedy

In a world that made the [Transformed](the-transformed) its rulers, the Ferals are the reminder that the [serum](apotheosis-serum) is not a gift it hands out cleanly. Every Feral went into her transformation as everyone else did and came out having lost the one thing it was never meant to take — and, for some, their very shape besides. The giantesses do not speak of them much. They are the price the serum rarely but reliably exacts, wandering the [Wilds](the-wilds) as proof that even the power that remade the world does not always know its own hand.

## Did You Know?

- Feralization is a **creation outlier**, like the [Tempered](tempered) — a rare random failure at the moment of transformation, *not* a consequence of size or anything the woman did.
- Most Ferals are ordinary-sized, commonly **12–15 feet** — a Feral can occur at any size, and is usually nowhere near [macro](macro) scale.
- The outlier strikes the **human** minority who take the [serum](apotheosis-serum) far more often than it strikes anthros.
- In some human cases the failure runs so deep it **reshapes the body too**, leaving a mindless recipient in an anthro form rather than a human one.
- A Feral is a mind *destroyed*; a [macro](macro) is a mind *remade but usually present*; an [Overgrown](the-overgrown) thing was *never a mind at all* — three entirely different things.
- [SinCorp](sincorp) marks Feral ground as off-limits even to its own researchers.
