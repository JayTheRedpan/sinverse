## Overview

R-Labs is the division of [SinCorp](sincorp) that handles the toys nobody else wants. Where the rest of the new world's toy economy is built around quality — prized companions, hardy wild-caught catches, useful servants — R-Labs is what exists at the other end: the destination for **bulk toys**, the surplus and the substandard, the males who are not good enough to sell, not suited to any role worth keeping them for, and too numerous to simply turn loose. What becomes of them is the division's business, and it is not a kind one.

To a toy, R-Labs is less a place than a threat. It is the thing whispered about, the fate held over the careless and the unlucky, the answer to where a toy goes when he is judged worth nothing — and the surest way to make a toy behave is to remind him that he could be *sent to R-Labs.*

## The Name

No one outside SinCorp knows what the "R" stands for, and SinCorp has never said.

The toys have supplied their own answers, and the two that have stuck tell you everything the division needs you to know. Some say it means **Reject** — the place the rejected go. Some say **Reduce** — for what is done to them there. SinCorp neither confirms nor denies, and the ambiguity is its own kind of cruelty: the name is left open precisely so that every toy who hears it can fill the blank with his own worst guess. Whatever the letter originally meant, if it ever meant anything at all, the rumours have long since made R-Labs mean *the end of the line.*

## What It Handles

R-Labs is where bulk toys are sorted, and a toy sorted into R-Labs has fallen out of every more valuable category. He is not a companion, not a useful servant, not a wild-caught prize, not deemed fit for any of the roles a city toy might occupy. He is overstock — a body in a population that, for all its decline, still produces more low-grade males than the economy above has any use for.

The division is, by turns, three things at once, and a toy who enters it can meet any of the three.

It is a **processing depot**: toys come in, are assessed, and are routed onward — a rare few judged worth enhancing with [SinCorp modifications](toy-modifications) and returned to the market, the rest sent down to the floor below.

It is a **research-supply operation**: cheap, abundant, low-value bodies are exactly what a SinCorp branch's undisclosed experiments run on, and R-Labs is a ready source of them, requisitioned in lots like any other raw material.

And it is a **permanent destination**: for most who arrive, R-Labs is simply where they end up, with no onward route at all. They are not sold, not freed, not used up in any particular project — only *kept*.

## The Bins

The signature of R-Labs, and the image that haunts the toys who fear it, is the bins.

The bulk toys who are neither enhanced nor consumed are **shrunk** — reduced with SinCorp's shrinking technology to a fraction of their former size — and stored, en masse, in clear lab bins: rows upon rows of transparent containers, each holding a teeming mass of tiny, living men. It is the most efficient way to warehouse a population that has been judged worthless but not quite disposable: hundreds to a bin, thousands to a shelf, a whole surplus reduced to inventory you can stack.

For the toys inside, it is existence narrowed to almost nothing — diminished past the point of any use, packed among others in the same state, kept alive by the division's care and for the division's reasons, with no role, no owner, and no way out. It is the literal bottom of the new world, where the word *toy* finishes the long journey from person to property to, finally, stock.

## The Sylph Caretakers

The toys in the bins are tended, and it is who tends them that gives R-Labs its particular edge. The caretakers of the shrunken are, very often, [Sylphs](project-sylph) — the lieutenant class SinCorp engineered from toys, set here to mind the tiny, reduced remnants of what they themselves once were.

It is not a gentle posting, and most who hold it are not gentle in it. A Sylph occupies a strange, narrow rung in the order: lifted out of toyhood but still beneath every giantess, granted authority but never security, forever proving she belongs above the things she came from. And there is no surer way to feel un-toy-like than to stand, enormous and in command, over the smallest and most wretched toys in existence — former brethren reduced to specks in a bin, entirely at her mercy. Many R-Labs Sylphs lord exactly that over their charges: cold, contemptuous, or outright cruel to the tiny men beneath them, putting as much distance as they can between what they are now and what lies teeming in the trays, precisely because that distance is so thin. The caretaker who is harshest with the bins is often the one most afraid of how recently he might have been in one.
