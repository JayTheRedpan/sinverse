## Overview

The **Transformed** — spoken of everywhere as the **giantesses** — are the women the [Apotheosis Serum](apotheosis-serum) remade, and the ruling class of the world that followed. They are the beings TDay was built to create: towering, powerful, all but ageless, and wired by the serum to stand at the top of the order it raised. Every other station in the new world is defined in relation to them. They are simply *the* class — the ones the world now belongs to.

To be Transformed is not a rank a woman earns or a role she takes. It is what the serum makes of any woman it touches, and after TDay it touched very nearly all of them. The giantesses are less a faction than a population: the overwhelming majority of the women left in the world, elevated together in a single day into something the old world had no place for.

## What the serum makes

A Transformed woman is the sum of the [serum's](apotheosis-serum) permanent work. The visible part is scale — most stand between **nine and twelve feet**, with the cities built to accommodate the range from the smaller **Amazon** tiers up to around thirty feet, and a rare few driven far past that into [macro](the-wilds) scale. With the size comes a powerfully built, extraordinarily durable body: old-world illness and injury scoured away, conventional weapons reduced to an irritant, and ageing all but halted, so that a giantess does not grow old on any timescale anyone has yet measured. The serum also rebuilds the body's sex, replacing whatever it began with in masculine form — a defining trait of the Transformed physique, catalogued in detail on the [serum's own page](apotheosis-serum).

But the change that truly *makes* a giantess is not physical. It is the rewrite of her empathy.

## The two-faced heart

The serum's most consequential effect is the one it performs on how a Transformed woman feels toward others — and it runs in two directions at once.

Toward **males**, it severs. A giantess's capacity to register a [toy's](toys) suffering as something that matters is cut at the root, and the void it leaves is wired straight into her sexual reward system, so that cruelty toward toys does not merely fail to trouble her but actively gratifies her — hardening over time from impulse into genuine need, the appetite [SinCorp](sincorp) researchers call **sadism deprivation**. This is why the order rests so easily on the giantesses: their dominance over toys is not a policy they enforce against their inclinations but the plain shape of their inclinations themselves.

Toward **other women**, the same rewrite runs in reverse, and intensifies. Transformed solidarity is fierce, warm, and almost proprietary. Giantesses cooperate, bond, form deep sisterhoods, and run whole [mega-cities](mega-cities) in concert without the rivalry the old world's powerful would have fallen into. A giantess who learns of a rare woman spared by TDay's first wave will move with startling urgency to find her and see her "ascended" — treating an untransformed sister not as a curiosity but as someone owed a birthright. The drive to gather every woman into the fold appears to be the very same circuitry that cancels her regard for males, simply pointed the other way.

The result is a being who can be tender and loyal to another woman in the same hour she treats a male as livestock, and feel no contradiction in it — because, to her remade mind, there is none.

## A sisterhood, not a hierarchy of women

It follows from the empathy rewrite that giantess society, among the women, is remarkably flat and cooperative. There is dominance everywhere in the new world, but among the Transformed it points only ever *downward*, at the toys. Between giantesses there is solidarity rather than competition — the unhurried confidence of a class that has everything it intends to have and shares it as peers.

This does not mean they are all equal in power. Some rise to rule; a [Matriarch](matriarchs) holds total authority over her city. But a Matriarch rules by command and by her grip on the order, not by lording scale or status over other giantesses, and the texture of the relationship between the Transformed is collegial where the relationship between a giantess and a toy is absolute. The pyramid of the new world has the giantesses across its whole top, side by side.

## The exceptions among them

Not every product of the serum fits the baseline, and the Transformed shade into several distinct populations that carry their own pages:

- The [**Tempered**](tempered) received the full transformation but kept their empathy — the rare women in whom the rewrite toward males never took. They are giantesses in body and outsiders in conscience.
- The [**macro**](the-wilds) are the outliers driven far past the norm by the unstable TDay Variant, often carrying the cognitive damage that comes with extreme scale, and living in the [Wilds](the-wilds) rather than the cities.
- The [**Ferals**](ferals) are the tragedies: Transformed whose minds the serum destroyed outright, left to instinct alone.
- The [**Sylph**](project-sylph), while not Transformed women themselves, are the engineered class set just beneath them — the one rung the giantesses answer *from above* rather than command directly.

The unmarked majority — coherent, city-dwelling, nine-to-twelve feet, empathy rewritten as designed — are what "giantess" means when the word is used without qualification.

## The long view

An all-but-ageless ruling class changes the character of the world in a way that is easy to overlook. The giantesses are not a generation that will pass and hand the order to a successor generation with different ideas. Barring violence, they simply *persist* — the same women, holding the same convictions, for a span no one can yet put a ceiling on. The new world is not a phase the giantesses are leading the world through. As far as anyone can tell, it is the world they will be living in indefinitely, and the permanence of the Transformed is much of why the order feels less like a regime than like a settled fact of nature.

## Did You Know?

- "Giantess" and "the Transformed" refer to the same class — the second is the neutral or clinical term, the first the everyday one.
- What most defines a giantess is not her size but her rewritten empathy: severed toward males and wired to reward cruelty, intensified toward women into fierce solidarity.
- Because dominance among the Transformed points only downward at toys, giantess society among the women is strikingly cooperative — sisterhood, not rivalry.
- The serum all but halts ageing. No Transformed woman has yet died of anything resembling old age, so the ruling class is, for now, effectively permanent.
- A giantess who hears of an untransformed woman surviving somewhere will often move urgently to find and "ascend" her — the same circuitry that cancels her regard for males, aimed the other way.
- The typical giantess stands nine to twelve feet; the cities are built for the full range up to around thirty, while the true [macro](the-wilds) are too large for city life at all.
