## Overview

The **Huntresses** — known across the new world as **the Paws** — are the giantesses who make a living venturing into the [Wilds](the-wilds) to capture toys and bring them back to the [mega-cities](mega-cities) for sale. They are not a company, a guild, or a SinCorp division. They are a loose sisterhood: a scattered fellowship of rangers bound by a shared craft, a shared danger, and an unwritten code, with no authority above any of them and no one's name on a roster.

What they do is among the most dangerous professions the new world offers, and among the most lucrative. A toy taken from the Wilds is the most prized catch in the trade, and the women willing to go in after one have made themselves indispensable to a world that wants what only they can fetch.

## A Loose Sisterhood

There is no head of the Paws, no ranks, no dues, no territory anyone is obliged to respect. To be a Paw is simply to do the work and to be known for doing it — and being *known* is the whole of the structure. Reputation is the only currency that matters among them: who brings back the best catches, who can be trusted at your back in the deep Wilds, who honours the code and who has broken it.

That code is unwritten and enforced by nothing but mutual regard, yet it holds remarkably well, because the Wilds punish those who have no one. A Paw who cheats a sister, abandons one in danger, or poaches a catch another was clearly working will find the others closed to her — no shared warnings, no help when something building-sized is bearing down, no one to band with for the hauls that need more than one. In a place that lethal, that exile is often a death sentence in slow motion. The sisterhood has no power to punish, and needs none.

What binds them instead is camaraderie and the hard respect of people who share an impossible job. They trade information, routes, and warnings; they drink and boast and mourn together between expeditions; and they hold a fierce, proprietary pride in being the only ones willing to go where they go.

## How They Hunt

A Paw's life alternates between two modes.

**Solo, for ranging.** Most of the actual hunting is done alone. A lone huntress can move quietly, cover ground, and slip through the Wilds without the noise and risk a group brings — and a single [wild toy](wild-toys), run down and bagged, is hers alone to sell. The patient, solitary stalk is the everyday work of the trade.

**Banded, for the big hauls.** But some jobs are too large or too dangerous for one. A settlement worth raiding, a stretch of Wilds thick with predators, a catch that needs cornering rather than chasing — for these, Paws band together into temporary packs, pooling their skills and their gear and splitting the take by whatever arrangement reputation and fairness dictate. These bands form and dissolve as the work demands; the same two huntresses might be partners one season and solo rivals the next, with no hard feeling in it. Banding up is also simple survival: in the deep Wilds, a sister at your back is the difference between a hard day and a fatal one.

## The Tools of the Trade

The Paws do not make their own equipment. They buy it from [SinCorp](sincorp), whose capture catalogue is built precisely for this work — incapacitating agents, pheromone lures and weapons, restraints scaled for a struggling toy, shrinking technology to make a catch portable, and the specialised gear that lets a giantess survive a country that wants her dead nearly as much as it wants her quarry. A well-equipped Paw is a walking advertisement for SinCorp's hunting line, and the relationship runs both ways: the huntresses are among SinCorp's best customers, and SinCorp's tools are much of what makes the profession survivable at all.

Skill still decides everything. The gear gets a huntress into the Wilds and gives her a chance against what lives there; it does not find the toy, read the country, or get her home alive. The best Paws are woodcraft and patience and nerve first, and customers of SinCorp second.

## The Prize

Wild toys are the most valuable catch in the entire trade, and the Paws exist because of it. A toy who has survived the Wilds is hardier, warier, and more resourceful than anything bred or kept in a city — and, crucially, he belongs to no one. He arrives at market unclaimed, his survival proof of a quality that commands a premium from buyers who want a toy with some fight and some story to him.

That premium is what justifies the risk. The Paws sell their catches into the cities' toy trade — to markets, to private buyers, to wealthy giantesses and the occasional [Matriarch](matriarchs) with a taste for a wild-caught toy — and a single good haul can be worth a season of danger. It also puts the Paws squarely on the other side of the Wild Toys' fragile freedom: to a toy scratching out a life in the open country, the Paws are the third terror, the one that is not the environment and not the apex predators, but a professional who has come all this way specifically for him.

## The Dangers

The Wilds do not distinguish between a huntress and her quarry. A Paw faces everything a wild toy faces — the [Overgrown](the-overgrown) in all its forms, from predatory flora to building-sized fauna to loosed SinCorp experiments — with the added distinction of being large enough to register as prey to the things that hunt at her scale. The [macro](macro) [Transformed](the-transformed) who rule the deep Wilds are a mortal danger to a Paw as much as to anyone; a huntress who blunders into a macro's territory, or stumbles across one of the [ferals](ferals), can become a catch herself. Much of the sisterhood's banding-up instinct comes from exactly this: the country is happy to eat the hunters too.

It is understood among them that not every Paw who walks into the Wilds walks back out, and that the ones who last are the ones who are careful, who are known, and who never go quite as deep as pride would tempt them to.

## Did You Know

- The Paws are not an organisation in any formal sense — there is no leader, no membership, and no roster. To be a Paw is simply to do the work and to be known among the others for doing it.
- Their entire structure rests on reputation and an unwritten code. The sisterhood has no power to punish a member who breaks it — but in the Wilds, being cut off from the others' aid and warnings is punishment enough.
- A wild-caught toy commands a premium over any city-bred or kept toy, because he is hardy, wily, and arrives unclaimed. The whole profession exists to chase that premium.
- The Paws are among SinCorp's best customers for capture equipment — and a living showcase for its hunting catalogue.
- They range solo but band into temporary packs for big or dangerous hauls, splitting the take by reputation and fair dealing rather than any fixed rule.
- A Paw is large enough to be prey herself in the deep Wilds. More than one has gone in after a toy and never come back out — taken by the very things she was hunting among.
