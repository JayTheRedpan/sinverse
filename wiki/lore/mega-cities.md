## Overview

The mega-cities are the dominant form of civilisation in the post-TDay world — purpose-built or radically restructured urban centres designed to accommodate the scale and needs of the Transformed. Each is an independent city-state ruled by a Matriarch, with its own culture, laws, and character.

## Formation

In the weeks following TDay, as the Chaos subsided, dominant Transformed individuals began consolidating control over territories. The largest concentrations of Transformed gathered in former urban centres, which were demolished and rebuilt, or expanded into entirely new configurations, to suit their inhabitants.

The process was not coordinated. Different mega-cities formed independently, which accounts for the significant variation in culture between them.

## Structure

There is no formal governing body that spans all mega-cities. Each Matriarch rules as she sees fit. Trade, communication, and the shared infrastructure of SinCorp create connections between cities, but political unity is not a value the post-TDay world has found reason to pursue.

Mega-cities are stratified:

- **The Transformed** occupy the dominant tier — full citizens with access to all city resources and services
- **Toys** (males) occupy no formal tier — they are not citizens. Their status within a given city depends on that city's culture, ranging from domestic servants to objects of entertainment to involuntary participants in SinCorp research

## Scale

Mega-city infrastructure is built to accommodate inhabitants ranging from 8 to approximately 30 feet. Standard doorframes, transit systems, furniture, and architecture all reflect this baseline. Macro-scale Transformed (>100ft) cannot practically live within the cities.

## Known Mega-Cities

- **Angora** — the most powerful, seat of Sin's authority and SinCorp's headquarters

*Additional mega-cities exist and are being documented.*

## Did You Know

- The term "mega-city" was not coined by any governing body — it emerged from informal usage in the months after TDay and was eventually adopted by SinCorp documentation without comment.
- A standard door in a mega-city is approximately 14 feet tall. Pre-TDay buildings that were not demolished had their doors and ceilings rebuilt first, before any other renovation work began.
- No two mega-cities have the same laws regarding toys. What is a minor infraction in one city may be the basis for a toy's confiscation in another.
- There are an estimated 12 to 15 established mega-cities worldwide. SinCorp has not released an official count.
- The smallest known mega-city has a Transformed population of approximately 800. The largest — Angora — has never officially published its population figures.
