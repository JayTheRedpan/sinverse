## Overview

Alea Panthera is the gambling capital of the new world — a [mega-city](mega-cities) built on chance, spectacle, and excess, where giantesses come from across the Wilds-spanning distances to lose themselves at the tables. Above, it glitters: a skyline of casinos and pleasure-houses, neon and gold, all the glamour the old world's great gambling cities ever had, rebuilt to giantess scale and stripped of any pretence of restraint. Below, in the lower districts, it is something else entirely — a noir underworld of black markets and back-alley dealers where the glittering city's appetites are quietly fed.

It is ruled by a single Matriarch, and it runs, top to bottom, on a single commodity that has become both its entertainment and its crisis: toys.

## A City Built on Chance

Alea exists for the wager. Nearly everything in it is staked, dealt, or bet on, and the city draws its visitors and its wealth from the simple, bottomless human appetite to gamble — an appetite the serum did nothing to diminish. By day and by night the casinos run, and a giantess can arrive flush and leave ruined, or arrive with nothing and leave a queen, on the turn of a card.

What sets Alea apart from any old-world gambling town is what sits in the pot. Money changes hands, of course — but in Alea, **toys are currency**. They function as chips at the lower tables, a living denomination passed across the felt; and at the high-stakes tables they are the prize itself, rare and valuable toys staked in pots that can turn a single hand into a fortune or a catastrophe. To win big in Alea is, very often, to win *toys* — and to lose is to lose them, along with everything else.

## The Scarcity

That a city should spend toys the way the old world spent coins has produced exactly the problem one would expect: in Alea, toys are running out.

Demand is bottomless and consumption is constant — gambled away, used up, broken in a city that churns through them faster than anywhere else in the new world. The local breeding facilities, drawing on SinCorp's vat-growing technology, cannot keep pace with Alea's appetite; though the global toy population long ago stabilized, no supply is bottomless enough for a city that spends them this fast, and the shortfall is felt here first and worst. The result is scarcity acute enough that the city has been forced to impose **sanctions** on toy use, something that would have been unthinkable in the early years, when a girl could grab a toy, use him to ruin, toss him in the bin, and reach for the next.

Those days are over in Alea. A toy is now an expensive thing that must be *kept* — fed, maintained, protected from breaking — and for many giantesses that is its own kind of trouble. The city has been dragged, by simple arithmetic, from disposable excess into a grudging, unnatural conservation, and the tension of that shift hums under everything.

## Getting a Toy

For an ordinary giantess in Alea, acquiring a toy has narrowed to a few hard options. She can pay the shops' inflated prices, if she can afford them. She can apply for a **hunting permit** and try to fill a weekly quota out in the Wilds herself — though most girls are not the hunter type and never were. Or, if she learns the right doors to knock on, she can take her chances in the **lower districts**, where the city's underground toy operations move fresh-caught and irregular merchandise at prices the legitimate shops can't match.

The gamble runs all the way down. A girl betting her last on a cheap black-market toy is making the same wager as the one at the high tables above her: win, and she has what she needs; lose, and she joins the ranks of the ruined. Alea has a grim local term for a giantess who gambles herself into total collapse — chasing toys and money she no longer has until something in her gives way. They say she has *gone [feral](ferals)*, though it is a ruin of desperation and addiction rather than the true serum-born feralism of the Wilds, and the lower districts are full of the women it has claimed.

## The Matriarch

Alea is the personal empire of its [Matriarch](matriarchs), [Meg](meg) — a great white shark giantess who ran the city's underworld long before she ever [transformed](the-transformed). In the old world she was a creature of the underground: rackets, fronts, and the quiet web of operations beneath a legitimate face. She is one still, simply at a scale the old world could never have given her. Notably small for a Matriarch, she has never needed size; she rules through nerve, cunning, and the predator's instinct that never lets her stop circling.

She came through [TDay](tday) with her wits intact long enough to claw her way to the top of the city she had always worked beneath, and she has ruled it ever since by doing exactly what she always did — running the rackets, working the angles, keeping the operations turning, and growing richer than she ever was before. Alea under Meg's rule is less a city governed than a business empire run: gambling, toy-trafficking, and the lucrative black markets beneath, all of it hers. She holds the city through the [power to command](matriarchs) it and the will to keep it firmly within the new order, and like any Matriarch she answers to no one above her save [Sin](sin).

## Traitors: The Owned Hunters

Alea's most distinctive institution is one the rest of the world keeps in the shadows: the **traitors**.

A traitor is a male toy who hunts other males. Such toys exist rarely across the new world — the occasional owner who discovers a toy with a knack for tracking, herding, and wrangling his own kind, and decides he is worth more loosed on a leash than broken on a bin — but most places that use them do so quietly, half-ashamed of the contradiction. Alea uses them openly. The Matriarch's underground operations run in large part on traitor labour: owned males, fitted with a marked collar that brands them protected property and grants them a sliver of independence, sent out to capture [wild toys](wild-toys) and run the lower-district markets that keep the city supplied.

A traitor's protection is real but conditional, and it is never *power*. He may stand above the toys he catches and sells; he may even talk back, within limits, to a girl who would otherwise break him. But his collar is his owner's to remove, his worth is hers to judge, and the moment a traitor's independence threatens the ideology of the era — that no male holds any real authority — those who indulge him are dealt with in extreme measures. A traitor lives in the narrow, precarious space between toy and tool: hated by his own kind as the gender-traitor he is, owned utterly by the mistress whose collar he wears, and alive only so long as he remains useful.

## A City of Appetite, Not Rivalry

For all its scheming, Alea is not a city of giantesses turning on one another. The wagers, the markets, the rackets, the scarcity — all of it points downward, at toys, never sideways at other girls. Giantesses come to Alea to indulge, to compete against the house and against chance, and to slake the appetites the serum left them; they do not come to one-up each other, and the city gives them no reason to. Its Matriarch's machinations are aimed at running her empire and keeping it supplied, not at any rival among the Matriarchs, with whom she shares the same easy mutual respect that holds between all the mega-cities. In Alea as everywhere, the dominant gender does not war with itself. The whole city is simply one enormous engine for indulging its appetites — and the only ones who suffer for it are the toys who are spent to feed them.

## Did You Know

- The city's name fits its trade: *alea* is an old-world word for dice and chance, and *Panthera* for the great cats — fitting for the new world's capital of gambling.
- In Alea, toys serve as literal gambling currency — chips at the lower tables and high-stakes prizes in the pots above — which is much of why the city burns through them faster than anywhere else.
- Alea has been forced to impose sanctions on toy use, a once-unthinkable measure born of an appetite that outruns even a stable supply. The disposable excess of the early era is simply no longer affordable here.
- A giantess who gambles herself into total ruin is said, in local slang, to have "gone feral" — a desperation-driven collapse distinct from the true serum-feralism of the Wilds.
- Alea is unusual for using **traitors** — owned male toys who hunt their own kind — openly, where most of the world keeps the practice in the shadows.
- A traitor's collar marks him as protected property, but the protection is entirely his owner's to revoke. No male in Alea holds real power, however long a leash he is given.
- Alea's Matriarch ran the city's underworld before TDay as well as after — the only thing her transformation changed was the scale of the empire she could build.
