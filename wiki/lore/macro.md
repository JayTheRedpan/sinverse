## Overview

**Macro** are the [Transformed](the-transformed) who grew far past the ordinary giantess norm — women risen to a scale measured not in feet but in the hundreds of them, too vast for any city built by anyone. They are the rare, extreme edge of what the [serum](apotheosis-serum) can do, and they are, quite simply, the largest things in the world.

Almost all of them are products of the unstable **[TDay](tday) Variant**, the formulation that drove its outliers to impossible extremes on the day itself. Where a typical giantess settled into nine to twelve feet, a macro kept growing — and the Variant that overgrew her body did not stop at her body.

## Scale beyond the cities

A macro cannot live where other giantesses live. The [mega-cities](mega-cities), built to accommodate the range up to around thirty feet, simply have no room for a being of macro scale; a doorway she could pass, a street she could walk, a building she would not level by leaning on it — none of it exists at her size. And so macro life is Wilds life, not by exile but by plain necessity. There is nowhere else that fits.

The rare exception is a matter of engineering, not welcome: a handful of mega-cities maintain **throughways of titanic scale** — corridors built deliberately to let a macro pass near or through without ruin — but these are unusual, and most cities simply keep their distance and hope the local macro keeps hers.

## The changed mind

The defining truth of the macro is not their size but what the size does to the mind. Reaching that scale rarely leaves a giantess as she was — and the change is not always *damage* in the simple sense. The Variant that reshaped a macro's body reshaped her mind alongside it, and the result is less often a mind broken than a mind **dramatically, unpredictably altered**.

What emerges varies enormously from one macro to the next. [Prima](prima) came out of it convinced she is a literal goddess, and carries herself accordingly. [Xena](xena) settled into something closer to an old-world warlord, a persona of conquest and dominion grafted onto her by the change. Others fixate, or swell with grandiosity, or reorganise themselves around some single towering idea. The through-line is that the mind at macro scale becomes *something else* — coherent, often, and all the more formidable for it, but rarely the woman who began the climb.

This should not be confused with the [Ferals](ferals), who are a different thing entirely. A Feral is a *creation outlier* — a mind destroyed at the moment of transformation, usually in an ordinary-sized woman and quite independent of scale. A macro's mind, by contrast, is very often *present*, merely remade by the strain of size into something vast and strange. The two do meet at the extreme edge — a macro degraded far enough by scale can arrive at an emptiness much like a Feral's, by a wholly different road — but most macro are not Ferals, and most Ferals are nowhere near macro scale.

## Territory

Macro do not gather. Each tends to claim her own **stretch of the [Wilds](the-wilds)** — a domain she rules and ranges across as its single apex, kept deliberately apart from the next macro's ground so that two titanic tempers rarely have cause to meet. The Wilds are enormous, and a world of macro is a world of separate kingdoms, each with one queen and no peers.

Some of these territories lie deep in the country; some press right up against the edges of the cities, which is where the rare titanic throughways come from, and where a city's unease runs highest.

## Tracked like weather

To a [Wild Toy](wild-toys), a macro is less a person than a phenomenon — and, like any phenomenon that can end you, one worth watching. Wild [Toys](toys) track the macro whose territory they share much the way the old world tracked storms: scouts note where she was and when, movement patterns are memorised and passed along, and a rough sense of *where she is likely to be* becomes a survival skill. It is deeply unreliable — a mind that vast and that strange keeps its own counsel — but an unreliable warning is still better than none, and knowing which horizon to keep an eye on has saved more Wild Toys than any weapon.

## Did You Know?

- A macro's mind is usually not *broken* but **transformed** — the Variant reshapes the mind as dramatically as the body, into something coherent but often wholly changed.
- [Prima](prima) believes herself a literal goddess and [Xena](xena) took on a warlord's persona — both examples of how differently the change can land from one macro to the next.
- Macro live in the [Wilds](the-wilds) out of pure necessity: no mega-city is built large enough to hold them.
- Each macro tends to claim her own separate territory, keeping apart from other macro — the Wilds are a patchwork of one-queen kingdoms.
- A few mega-cities build **titanic throughways** to let a macro pass without destruction, but this is rare; most simply keep their distance.
- Wild Toys track a local macro's movements like weather — through scouts and memorised patterns — an unreliable but life-saving practice.
- Macro are not [Ferals](ferals): a Feral is a mind *destroyed* (a size-independent creation outlier), while a macro's mind is usually *present*, merely remade by scale — though extreme scale can blur the two.
