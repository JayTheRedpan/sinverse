## Overview

The Resistance is the organised opposition to the new world order — a loose, leaderless network of [Tempered](tempered) women and toys working to get males out of the [mega-cities](mega-cities) and into the relative freedom of the [Wilds](the-wilds). It is not an army, not a government-in-exile, and not a movement with a manifesto. It does not seek to reverse [TDay](tday), de-transform [the Transformed](the-transformed), or retake the world. It has something far narrower and more achievable: a list of people who need to be somewhere else, and the means to sometimes get them there.

In a world built on the certainty that toys are property, the Resistance is the standing assertion that they are people. That assertion is dangerous enough that the organisation has shaped its entire existence around surviving it.

## Structure

The Resistance has no central leadership, no headquarters, and no roster. It exists as a scattering of independent **cells** — small, self-contained, and operated with full autonomy — that coordinate with one another only when a job requires it, and then at significant risk. A cell knows its own people and its own routes, and as little as possible about anyone else's. When a cell is exposed, it falls alone; it cannot give up names it never knew.

This is by hard-won design rather than ideology. The earliest Resistance efforts tried for centralised coordination, and [SinCorp](sincorp) infiltrated and dismantled them within months. What survived the cull was the part that could not be unravelled by pulling a single thread — the cellular structure that remains the organisation's defining feature and its single greatest defence. The Resistance is difficult to destroy for the same reason it is difficult to organise: there is no centre to strike, and nothing to seize that would bring down the rest.

It also keeps no records. No archives, no operation logs, no ledgers of who was moved or where they went. Everything that matters lives in trusted memory, because anything written down is a thing that can be found.

## Members

**The Tempered.** The [Tempered](tempered) — women who took the full transformation but kept their empathy — are the organisation's moral core. Built like the new world's apex and unable to stop caring about its victims, they tend to have nowhere else to put what they feel, and so the rare Tempered who surfaces drifts, sooner or later, toward this work. Within a cell a Tempered is often the organising force and, crucially, an asset nothing else can replace: a giantess who can pass unsuspected among the Transformed, move freely through the cities, and turn a giantess's size and access against the order rather than for it. The toys who work alongside them sometimes call them **Anchors**.

**Toys.** Males who have already gotten out and choose to go back in. Some return for loved ones still inside. Some do it because they have nowhere else to direct what was done to them. All of them understand, precisely, how it ends if they are caught — and go anyway. They are the Resistance's hands inside the cities, in the places a giantess, Tempered or not, cannot easily reach.

## What They Do

The Resistance's work is unglamorous by design. Its tools are smuggling routes and safe houses in the border zones between city and Wilds; forged [SinCorp documentation](sincorp) where a city's culture demands papers; quiet intelligence on SinCorp activity and on which toys are under immediate threat; and the patient logistics of moving a person from inside the order to outside it without anyone noticing he is gone. The most successful extractions are never the dramatic ones — they are paperwork, timing, and an unremarkable vehicle making an unremarkable journey.

What the Resistance is *for*, beneath all of it, is the toys themselves. Not the population as a number — that cratered after TDay and then stabilized, and SinCorp's vats hold it steady now — but the people inside it: an entire class born, bought, spent, and owned, with no say in any of it. The Resistance regards that as the core urgency of everything it does — not an abstract injustice but particular lives held as property, freed one at a time, with extraction the only lever it actually holds.

## The Underground Railroad

The Resistance's smuggling routes are spoken of, collectively, as an underground railroad — a chain of safe houses, handoffs, and guided crossings that moves a freed toy from the heart of a mega-city out through the border zones and into the Wilds. Each link knows only the links it touches; no one holds the whole route, which is exactly what keeps the route alive.

Where the railroad ultimately reaches toward is [Bastion](bastion) — the rumoured permanent toy city hidden deep in the Wilds, the one place a freed toy can go and actually stay free. The Resistance does not run Bastion; Bastion is independent, and governs itself. But it is the destination that gives the whole dangerous enterprise its point. A toy pulled out of a city has to have somewhere to land, and Bastion — real, deniable, and never quite confirmed even to the people steering toward it — is the somewhere. The Resistance moves people toward a place it cannot promise exists, on faith that it does.

## The Adversary

The Resistance's primary adversary is [SinCorp](sincorp), the one institution present in every city and the architect of the order the Resistance works against. SinCorp dismantled the early Resistance, tracks the Tempered the organisation depends on, and stands behind the documentation, surveillance, and infrastructure the cells must constantly work around.

What SinCorp has *not* done is mount an all-out suppression campaign — and the reason is telling. To pursue the Resistance at full scale would mean acknowledging, openly, that it is large enough to require pursuing: that the order is not as total or as universally accepted as it presents itself. SinCorp has apparently judged the admission more costly than the nuisance. The Resistance survives, in part, inside that calculation — too widespread to quietly erase, too inconvenient to loudly chase, tolerated in the shadows by an adversary that would rather the world not know it is there at all.

## Did You Know

- The Resistance has no name for itself. "The Resistance" is what others call it; individual cells use their own internal identifiers, or none at all.
- Its cellular structure was not chosen on principle but forced by survival — every attempt at centralised organisation was infiltrated and destroyed by SinCorp early on.
- The Resistance keeps no archives of any kind. No member lists, no operation records, no ledgers. Everything lives in trusted memory, because anything written can be found.
- SinCorp knows the Resistance exists and has chosen not to pursue large-scale suppression — presumably because doing so would mean admitting the Resistance is large enough to need it.
- The Tempered within the Resistance are sometimes called **Anchors** by the toys who work beside them — not an official designation, just something that stuck.
- The most effective extractions are the least dramatic: forged paperwork, careful timing, and an unremarkable journey no one thinks to question.
- The Resistance does not aim to undo TDay or de-transform anyone. Its entire programme is getting specific people to somewhere safer — most often toward Bastion.
