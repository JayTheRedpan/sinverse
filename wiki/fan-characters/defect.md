# Overview:   
  
Defect is not a name so much as a designation assigned to a specific clone series owned and manufactured by Sincorp. The original “Defect”, was an unusual red squirrel captured by Sincorp Huntresses and sequestered in a lab. He displayed intense devotion to the large women which only intensified as they abused him. Samples were collected prior to his eventual death at the hands (or rather, feet) of an over-enthusiastic scientist. Vivette Venenatus—a Sincorp Toy Engineer—tested the toyline extensively, and pushed for mass rollout despite moderate opposition.   
  
His insistent and maniacal dedication to his owner regardless of the abuses afflicted is offputting to some women, though a minority of the girls find that watching a toy continue to lavish adoration as they tear him apart has an intoxicating quality. This has led to a moratorium on the most powerful durability modifications for his series, over fears that overuse of a Defect might induce a feral descent in a small subset of the girls.

## Appearance:   
  
In many ways, the Defect series presents as an average “male”, a toy of average height, a slightly thinner than average build, and a slightly larger than average penis, though it rarely sees use.   
  
His rusty red coat covers most of his body and tail, slightly brighter at the ears, tail, and crest of his head, and from his jaw to his crotch sits a patch of thinner, ruddy beige fur.   
  
Individual clones often bear distinct scars bestowed by enthusiastic owners, and as the Defect series is forbidden certain stronger modifications, those scars remain permanent.   
  
## Personality:   
  
In another society, Defect would be considered extroverted, enthusiastic and cheery to a fault. In the world of Sinverse, these traits manifest as an unyielding need to please his mistresses through any means possible, that need only reinforced by every inflicted wound. There are innumerable tales of Defects thanking their mistresses even as their legs are torn away and devoured, or cute stories of a Defect begging his mistress to crush his skull underfoot in the hopes it would put a smile on her face. Every woman is his God, and Defect’s devotion knows no limits.   
  
There is a small caveat. If, for some perverse reason, a Defect series clone is treated too kindly for too long, he will begin actively seeking abuse up to self-termination at the whim of the nearest woman. He displays visceral discomfort when asked to put himself before any woman, and will aggressively reject further attempts to “pleasure” him until he feels he has received a commensurate level of abuse. This is a known quality, and seems as immutable as the rising sun despite many attempts to warp his sense of self.   
  
Defect does not always get along with other toys, as he fervently believes all of toykind belongs beneath womankind. This attitude is generally not well received among toys.   
  
## History:   
  
### Pre-TDay -   
  
Little is known about the original Defect. When attempting to clone the line with prior memories intact, a form of obfuscating “corruption” becomes apparent. The clone will show signs of severe distress when unable to recall pertinent details from his previous life, terribly upset at the idea of failing his mistress.   
  
The few details that have been gleaned are as follows:   
He existed prior to TDay, and was never a wild-born toy. Through either luck or profession he was not caught in the initial slaughter, isolation granting him a measure of protection. How an individual with his disposition towards the massive women of the world survived for more than a minute on TDay is still poorly understood, but it is theorized that a specific encounter or pheromone unlocked his latent absurdly submissive streak. The early days of huntress activity are not well documented.   
  
### Post-TDay -   
  
The original Defect is long gone, obliterated and lost to the ether. All subsequent clones exist solely as property of their respective owners. There is no “mainline” clone, though a discerning eye will find Defects in places that cater to a specific brand of sadist. A clone named “D”, belonging to Shari Katili, is suspected to have the current record for longest continued survival, much to his chagrin.   
  
## As a Toy:   
  
Defects are occasionally given tasks beyond personal attendant and sex object, however their obsessive devotion presents an unusual challenge.   
  
While they will do anything to please their mistress, they have an obsessive need to please any woman, making them unreliable for jobs that might require them to refuse a customer based on many factors. They also tend to encourage violent treatment, leading to an overall higher fatality rate and excessive cleanup budget.   
  
Occasionally used as food, though the nature of the toy encourages binge eating as he will feed himself to the giant in droves when shrunken to snack size.   
  
## Relationships:   
  
Defects tend not to survive long enough to form lasting relationships. As a clone, each Defect does maintain distinct, individual connections, and gradually develop little idiosyncrasies, often related to their owner.   
  
Two notable relationships for different Defects are Vivette Venenatus—the timber-rattlesnake engineer responsible for the Defect series production line, and Shari Katili—an iridescent navy-black panthress who has formed what some view as an “unhealthy” attachment to her primary Defect.   
  
In both cases, each woman spends their entire toy allotment on versions of Defects, though Vivette likes to keep a small team around for tasks, each member of which she might use up at any moment, and Shari likes to splurge and use all save her primary Defect up in a gruesome orgy, forcing him to witness a fate she never intends to grant.

## Preferences:   
  
Defect’s preference is abuse, in the simplest terms, and while he would never challenge his mistress in the hope that she might batter him out of frustration, he will relentlessly and happily recommend ways his suffering might alleviate any small inconvenience for his owner.   
  
He has shown a predilection towards foot-themed domination, but any form of cruelty will be gratefully accepted, so long as the perpetrator is a female.   
  
He is not averse to male sexual encounters, but any sense of submission evaporates. He can be coerced to perform in either role if it is at the request of a dominant woman, and will do so with the appropriate gusto of one seeking approval.   
  
## Usage Guide:   
  
Defect must always be shown in obedience to his master, though his relentless enthusiasm can sometimes cause him to misinterpret orders.   
  
Defect is always cheery and helpful, regardless of his level of physical suffering. He will often recommend things that would explicitly kill him, without batting an eye.   
  
Defect is disposable by nature, and may die in any number of stories. There are no off-limit kinks for his stories, as long as they are performed by or at the behest of a dominant woman.   
  
Defect will react uncomfortably to excessive kindness from his superiors.   
  
## Did You Know:  
* Defect seems to have instinctual knowledge of pressure points and reflexology, every clone doubling as a surprisingly effective masseuse.   
* Defect series clones are currently prohibited from receiving full-strength Second Wind modifications, out of fear that it could prolong a dangerous state of feral ecstasy some women experience.   
* The current record of survival for a single Defect is one year, four months and twelve days, owned by Shari Katili. No other Defect has lived beyond seven months.   
* Defect series clones will often self-terminate via the nearest giantess when they receive kindness in significant excess of abuse.   
* Defect series clones are seen as inferior toys by many women, as their response to suffering can feel lackluster for those who do not appreciate devotion.  
* There have been several Resistance attempts to “rescue” a Defect, though all but one have failed near immediately. The most successful involved a rare, abandoned Defect, and still ultimately failed due to the intervention of Alevia.
