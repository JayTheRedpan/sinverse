## Overview

Vex is a sabertooth-jackal hybrid and one of Angora's most feared private dominatrices — and, to one young giantess, something stranger than a reputation: the closest thing [Riley](riley) has to family. She is the vicious sabertooth of Riley's account, the "aunt" who took the young giantess in and shaped her, feeding her disgust at her own parentage into the settled hatred Riley carries to this day.

She stands around eighteen feet, black-furred and lit all over in a vivid, unnatural neon green, and she is known less for what she does to a toy's body than for what she does to its mind. Vex is a dominatrix of the psychological school: the physical side of domination is a given, almost beside the point. What she prizes is the slow, theatrical work of reducing a toy to something small, pathetic, and wholly dependent on her — and she keeps to Angora, where a reputation like hers can be indulged in full.

For all her command, Vex is not solitary. Her partner is the snow leopard macro [Mia](mia), a giantess many times her size who answers to no one in the world but her — a dominant to all creation who is, for Vex alone, entirely a sub. Their bond predates everything the new world made of them, and it has outlasted the enormous gulf of scale the transformation opened between them.

## Appearance

Vex is a striking double hybrid of sabertooth cat and jackal, standing around eighteen feet — the heavy, apex build and long killing canines of the sabertooth crossed with the sharper muzzle, tall ears, and lean lines of the jackal. Her fur is a deep, uniform black, which serves mostly as the backdrop for the thing that makes her unmistakable: an electric, wholly unnatural neon green that runs through her hair and surfaces again everywhere the eye travels — her eyes, her nose, the pads of her paws, her claws, and a scattering of markings tracing across her body. Against all that black, the green reads less like a colour than like something lit from within.
 
She dresses the part she has chosen for herself. Latex is her signature — a glossy, severe wardrobe that frames her rather than conceals her and leaves no question about the role she means to play. The serum left her endowed, as it does the Transformed, in an equine form; like the rest of her, it is the same vivid green.


## Personality

Vex's cruelty is a craft, and its medium is the mind. She is not, at heart, a woman chasing her own gratification — the physical side of what she does interests her mainly as a tool. What absorbs her is the *effect* she can produce in a toy: the embarrassment, the shrinking, the moment a proud thing starts to believe it really is as small and pathetic as she insists. She works toward that patiently and with open relish, and counts an encounter a success not by what she took but by how thoroughly she has hollowed out the toy's sense of itself, until little is left but a broken, doting pet.

She is theatrical about it, and exacting. She expects to be addressed as **mistress**, and the insistence is less vanity than method — every "mistress" is one more brick pulled from a toy's dignity and handed to her. Toward the world above her toys she is composed, confident, and coolly amused, a woman entirely at ease with her repute and in no hurry to soften it. The places her hardness gives way to something softer are few and chosen: Riley, the young giantess she took in and shaped, and [Mia](mia), her partner — with whom the dominance she imposes on the rest of the world becomes something shared rather than inflicted, a thing the two of them turn outward together.

## History

### Before TDay

Vex was one of Sin's earliest works. Before the world ever knew what was coming, she was among the first women Sin transformed — a giantess made in the quiet years of Sin's Rise, in a [SinCorp](sincorp) laboratory, while the serum was still a secret and its maker still building toward TDay. She appears in the origin-story comic **Sinverse Rise**, which shows those early transformations and places Vex among the very first of the new world's giants, walking it while it was still hidden inside the old one.

That head start shaped her. Where most of the Transformed woke into the new order all at once, in the Chaos of TDay, Vex had already been what she was for some time — one of the reasons her command of the role is so total, and so unbothered.

She did not come to it alone. She and [Mia](mia) were partners already, in the old world, before either of them was anything but ordinary — and the transformation, for all it remade them, did not touch that. Vex changed first, in the lab, years ahead of her partner; Mia would follow on TDay itself, and come out of it something else entirely. That the two of them held together across that gap — first the years Vex spent transformed while Mia was not, then the vast difference of scale that TDay opened between them — is the quiet, defining fact of both their lives.

### After TDay

When TDay finally came and the world tipped into the shape Sin had built for it, Vex was already at home in it. She settled in [Angora](angora), close to the seat of the world's power, and made a name for herself as a private dominatrix of vicious reputation.

At some point — how, exactly, has never been established — the young giantess [Riley](riley) came under her wing: the estranged offspring of the toys [Jay](jay) and [Libby](libby), set on Vex's path for reasons that were likely never wholly anyone's to decide. Vex took her in and shaped her, and whatever guardianship she brought to it ran through the familiar channel: she worked on Riley deliberately, stoking her shame at her toy parentage into the hard, settled hatred that defines her today. To Riley, she is, and remains, "aunt."

## Preferences

Vex's primary kink is domination, specifically psychological. She derives immense satisfaction from not only physically overwhelming her toys but also shattering their mental composure until they are reduced to mere vessels of submission and need. Her dominance isn't merely about exerting control; it is about obliterating the sense of self in those she subjugates, transforming them into brain broken pets who exist solely for her pleasure.

She delights in the verbal degradation of her partners, relentlessly chipping away at their psyche until they are little more than extensions of her will. Vex prefers a power dynamic where her toys must address her as "mistress" and understand that their entire existence revolves around pleasing her every whim. Her approach is methodical: she begins with physical dominance, using her towering height and immense strength to reinforce the helplessness of those beneath her. Then, through verbal abuse and mental manipulation, she strips away any remaining sense of self-worth or dignity in her toys.

Vex's equine endowment serves as both a tool for sexual domination and a symbol of her power over her partners. She revels in its use not just to satisfy her own libido but to assert her dominance, ensuring that her toys understand their place beneath her. Her enjoyment stems from the feeling of complete control—where the toy's pleasure is entirely at her discretion. This emotional and physical subjugation is what truly elates Vex; the power to shape another being into a compliant, submissive tool brings her deep satisfaction.
